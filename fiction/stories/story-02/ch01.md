# Chapter 1

Thessaly Vane was early.

That was the first wrong thing. Covenant representatives didn't arrive early to meetings in taverns. They arrived precisely when they intended to, usually after the other party had been waiting long enough to feel the imbalance. Mira had seen Thessaly do it twice now — that measured entrance, the unhurried survey of the room, the way she settled into a chair as though she'd selected it from a catalog of better options and found this one merely adequate.

Tonight she was already seated when Mira came through the door. Corner table, back to the wall, a cup of tea untouched in front of her. Her coat was road-dusted. Her hair, usually pinned with architectural precision, had a strand loose at the temple.

Thessaly Vane had hurried. That was the second wrong thing.

"Mira." A nod, not a greeting. "Sit."

Mira sat. She caught Hesper's eye at the bar and got a small shake of the head — *she's been here twenty minutes, ordered tea, hasn't touched it, hasn't talked to anyone.* Hesper Brack read people the way most people read broadsheets, and her assessments were free if you knew how to ask.

"I have a job," Thessaly said. "Time-sensitive. I need your Society mobilized by morning."

"Good evening to you too." Mira unbuttoned her jacket and leaned back, letting the chair take her weight. The Wet Ember was warm tonight — the coal fire doing its work, the gas lamps throwing steady amber light across the worn wood of the bar. Two factory workers arguing about the prior season's boxing standings near the door. A Greycoat inspector she didn't recognize nursing a whiskey alone. The usual crowd, minus anyone who mattered. "Morning is soon. What's the rush?"

Thessaly folded her hands on the table. Her rings caught the firelight — silver, Covenant sigils, the kind of jewelry that was decorative in the same way a uniform was decorative. "Six days ago, a Sootborn trader arrived in the northeast market to purchase supplies. Rope, lanterns, preservation jars. Field equipment. She was overheard discussing what she called 'the site' with a merchant she apparently trusted." Thessaly's mouth thinned. "The merchant's discretion was less reliable than the trader assumed."

"Overheard by whom?"

"By enough people that the information reached us through three independent channels within forty-eight hours."

Mira did the math. Three channels meant the rumor was already wide. If it had reached the Covenant through three sources, it had reached other ears through others. "What's the site?"

"A structure. In the Wilds, northeast of the city — near a Sootborn settlement on the forest edge. The trader described it as pre-Tear construction of unknown origin. Sealed. The Sootborn explorers who found it were apparently unable to enter fully, but they saw enough to report something significant inside." Thessaly paused. "A container. The descriptions are vague and secondhand, but the Covenant's assessment is that this warrants immediate investigation."

"Vague and secondhand," Mira repeated. "You're sending us into the Wilds on marketplace gossip."

"I'm sending you into the Wilds on the best intelligence available, which is imperfect. The Covenant is aware of the limitations." Thessaly's composure didn't crack, but something shifted behind her eyes — a flicker of the urgency she was working to contain. "We are not the only party who received this information. I have reason to believe the Ashworth Collective is also acting on it. If they reach the site first — if they recover the artifact and bring it within the Collective's control — the Covenant loses access permanently."

There it was. Not a careful research expedition. A race.

Mira studied Thessaly's face in the firelight. The Covenant of Embers was Awakening-leaning — they believed the Aether mattered, that it was revelation, not disease. An artifact from a pre-Tear structure was exactly the kind of thing that fed their worldview: evidence that the forces at work in the world predated the catastrophe everyone blamed them for. Proof, maybe, that the Veil had been thinning for longer than anyone admitted.

That was worth hurrying for. That was worth sending a Society that had completed exactly one job into territory none of them had ever worked.

"Why us?" Mira asked. Not fishing for compliments. Doing the math.

Thessaly had the grace not to dress it up. "You can leave immediately. Your Society has no prior obligations that would delay deployment. And the Covenant's other assets are committed to work that cannot be interrupted on short notice."

*You're available and expendable.* Mira heard the translation clearly enough. She didn't take it personally. It was honest, and honest was more useful than flattering.

"The pay?"

Thessaly named a figure. It was good — better than the Harker job, with a bonus structure for confirmed recovery. Enough to keep the Ledger comfortable for two months, maybe three if Mira was careful.

"We'll need supplies. Field gear for Wild Zone work. Provisions for — how far is this settlement?"

"Two days' travel on the northeast road, based on the trader's route."

"Two days to get there. Unknown time in the Wilds. Two days back. Call it a week, assuming nothing goes wrong." Mira almost smiled. Things always went wrong. "We can be on the road by first light."

Thessaly nodded once. She reached into her coat and produced a sealed envelope — the Covenant's formal commission, signed and stamped, authorizing Ash & Veil Recovery to act on their behalf. The wax seal was still warm. She'd prepared it before coming.

"One more thing," Thessaly said as Mira took the envelope. "The Sootborn settlement is not a Covenant interest. The people there have no obligation to assist you, and the Covenant makes no claim on their cooperation. How you navigate that relationship is your professional judgment."

Which meant: *we know they won't help you and we're not going to pretend otherwise.*

Mira pocketed the commission. "I'll send word when we're back."

"I'll be waiting." Thessaly stood, collected her coat, and left without touching her tea. Hesper watched her go with the expression she reserved for people who ordered drinks they didn't intend to consume.

---

The flat on Harrier Street was two rooms above a cobbler's shop, reached by an exterior staircase that groaned in weather and creaked in calm. The Society had been here for three months. It smelled like leather, lamp oil, and the faint copper sweetness that drifted east from the Quarter on warm evenings.

Kael was cleaning his revolver at the kitchen table when Mira came in. Aldric was reading in the corner chair, a book balanced on his knee and a cup of tea actually being drunk, which put him one up on Thessaly. Sera was cross-legged on the floor near the window, her back against the wall, writing in the journal she never let anyone else touch.

Three faces looked up. Mira hung her jacket on the hook by the door.

"We have a job," she said. "First light. Pack for a week in the field."

Kael set down the cleaning rod. "Where?"

"Northeast. Past the farmland, into the Wild edges. There's a Sootborn settlement near the tree line — two days on the road. Beyond that, something in the Wilds that the Covenant wants found."

"Something," Aldric said. He closed his book, keeping his place with one finger. "That's specific."

"A structure. Pre-Tear, apparently. The Sootborn found it and word got out. The Covenant wants us to locate it and recover an artifact from inside." Mira pulled a chair from the table and sat, feeling the day's tension settle into her shoulders now that she'd stopped moving. "The intelligence is thin. Marketplace rumors, traced back to a Sootborn trader who talked too freely. But the Covenant is treating it as credible."

Sera looked up from her journal. "The Wilds. The actual Wilds?"

"The edges. Near the settlement, not the deep forest."

"That's still —" Sera stopped herself, but her eyes were bright. Not fear. Something hungrier. "I've never been in a real Wild Zone. Not a strong one."

"You'll get your chance." Mira looked at Kael. "The Covenant thinks the Ashworth Collective heard the same rumors. If they've dispatched Ironwork, they have a head start or they're right behind us. Speed matters."

Kael's expression didn't change, but his hand moved to rest on the revolver he'd been cleaning. A reflex, not a decision. "Rules of engagement?"

"This is a recovery job, not a fight. We find the site, we assess the artifact, we bring back what we can and report what we can't." Mira held his gaze. "But we're operating outside city limits, in territory with no Greycoat presence, against a rival Society backed by the Collective. So pack accordingly."

Aldric had taken off his spectacles and was polishing them — the thing he did when he was processing. "Pre-Tear construction," he said quietly. "In the Wilds. That's — the Wilds were settled land before the Tear. Farms, villages. Anything pre-Tear out there would have been swallowed when the zone expanded. For it to be accessible now, the zone would have to have shifted, exposed it. Or it was never buried in the first place."

"The Sootborn explorers got inside," Mira said. "Partially. They found something they couldn't retrieve and came back for supplies. That's when the information leaked."

"What kind of artifact?" Aldric asked.

"A container. That's all we have."

Aldric put his spectacles back on. "I'll need to bring the full kit. Measurement instruments, documentation materials. If this is genuinely pre-Tear, the site itself may be as significant as whatever is inside it."

Mira nodded. She looked around the room — Kael already calculating logistics, Aldric already thinking about research methodology, Sera already feeling the pull of the Aether that waited at the end of the road. Three people she'd chosen, doing exactly what she'd chosen them for.

She didn't tell them about the subtext. The expendability. The sense that the Covenant was throwing them at a long shot because the cost of failure was a Society nobody would miss. That calculation was hers to carry, and she carried it the way she carried everything — quietly, and without asking for help.

"First light," she said. "Travel light, move fast, and don't talk about where we're going until we're past the city limits. I don't want to find out who else is listening."

The flat settled into the focused quiet of preparation. Kael reassembled his revolver with the mechanical ease of a man who could do it blindfolded. Aldric opened his satchel and began extracting instruments, laying them on the table in rows for inspection. Sera closed her journal and went to the window, looking east toward the Quarter, where the air was thick and sweet and the shadows moved on their own.

Somewhere out there, past the farms and the coaching inns and the last ward-posted village, the Wilds waited. And in the Wilds, something that shouldn't exist sat in a structure that nobody built, sealed behind defenses that nobody understood, holding answers to questions that nobody had thought to ask yet.

Mira pulled the Ledger from the shelf and noted the commission. The numbers were good. For now, that was enough.
