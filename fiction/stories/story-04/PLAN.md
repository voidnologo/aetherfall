# Story 04 — Plan

## Working Title: No Kinder Country

## Overview

The Covenant of Embers reads Mira's debrief on the Collective's planned mill expedition and commissions the Society to delay or disable the convoy. The job cannot be traced back to the Covenant — it must be deniable action. The Sootborn community at the Wild edge, whose ancestral land the convoy route crosses, wants the expedition stopped: they moved out there to escape the factions and have no interest in hosting a militarized push through their country. They won't fight, but they will help — local knowledge, hidden trails, refuge.

The Society isn't a combat troop. They're a planner, a scholar-healer, a marksman, and a wild caster. What they bring is strategy. The plan: a commando-style strike-and-run ambush at a natural chokepoint — the ford across the Old Cart Road where the convoy must string out to cross. Three phases — strike, sabotage, withdrawal — with contingencies, an abort signal, and withdrawal paths pre-walked by the Sootborn.

The ambush works. The cost is terrible. Kael's revolver misfires at the worst moment (Aether/Galvanic zone interference — the Story 03 seed paying off), he takes a through-and-through bullet wound to the side, and the subsequent blast from his own charges throws him into stone, breaking ribs and compounding the injury. He goes down with a death timer running. Aldric drops his active strong-tier Barrier — the structural centerpiece of the whole ambush — and sprints through fire and gunfire to stabilize him. The Mend he casts in field conditions, at the strong tier his reserves will bear, is stabilization, not healing. Kael survives, barely. The Society withdraws along Sootborn trails, Kael on a stretcher, and recovers for eight days at the settlement.

They return to Ashwick with the mission complete: the convoy destroyed, the expedition postponed two months. The Covenant is pleased. Thessaly warns Mira privately that the Collective knows who did it — they don't have evidence, but they know. Retribution is coming in the form of agents, assassins, or observers. Mira absorbs the warning and doesn't share it with the team that night. At Harrier Street, she notices small discontinuities: a lock with picking scratches, a drawer slightly moved, a window catch re-engaged by a stranger's hand, a neighbor's story about two men "from the Greycoat Authority" doing a welfare check, a shape in the alley opposite that doesn't move. She files them. She doesn't raise them. The team rests. The compass on the coat hook spins once in the still air and settles. The flat feels like home. The shadow outside doesn't move.

## Thematic Focus: Combat as Lived Experience

Story 04 is the "combat story." Its secondary purpose — invisible to the reader, primary for the design — is to bring the game's combat mechanics to life as narrative. The Society isn't a military unit, and the story foregrounds this: their real weapon is strategy and their real victory is getting home. Every combat moment should make a reader who later picks up the rulebook think: "Oh — *that's* what a firefight feels like when your team is four people who barely know how to be a team yet."

**Planning as power.** The Society's pre-combat preparation is the longest sustained showcase: the terrain read, the three-phase structure, the contingencies, the abort signal, the withdrawal paths. A good plan buys time and buys lives. A bad plan gets people killed. The team is good at planning and the story rewards that.

**Combat casting vs. exploration casting.** Story 03 showed magic in a contemplative, exploratory frame. Story 04 shows magic in chaos: Aldric holding a strong-tier Barrier for three minutes through gunfire; Sera's casting variance amplified by Aether pulses and panic; the uncontrolled surge when Kael falls; scholarly tier-choice under time pressure; wild-caster exhaustion cascading faster than she can track.

**Weapons as environment-dependent tools.** Kael's rifle works at range. His knife works at touch. His revolver, in a zone where the Aether is stacking, misfires at the worst moment and costs him. The Story 03 seed — "his revolver feels wrong in Aetheric territory" — pays off viscerally. Weapons aren't neutral objects; they're engineering that depends on the zone they're in.

**Galvanic suppression gear.** The Collective's answer to magic — field dampeners, resonance disruptors, portable dead-zone generators — gets named, described, and targeted. The ambush's sabotage objective is specifically to destroy this equipment. The story shows what anti-magic technology looks like, why it matters, and how the Society deals with it (by staying out of effective range, and by blowing it up).

**The death timer and the dropped action.** The mechanical centerpiece. When a character goes critical, they have a window before they die. Stabilization requires action. Aldric's decision to drop the Barrier — abandoning the tactical structure of the whole ambush — to sprint to Kael is the emotional fulcrum of the story. It is also the exact mechanic expressed in narrative: *stop what you were doing, because a friend is dying*.

**Injury persistence and recovery timelines.** Kael doesn't spring back. Neither does Sera. Neither does Aldric. The eight-day Sootborn recovery arc is the story forcing the reader to feel that combat has durable cost — not a scene-change reset.

**Bonds revealed, not declared.** The Society has been together less than a year. They haven't done this before — this specific kind of near-loss. The story is also the story of how the bonds they've been building reveal their actual weight. Not through speeches. Through Aldric's unconscious decision to drop the Barrier. Through Kael's "thank you" and Aldric's "anytime." Through Sera's head on Kael's shoulder at the common-house fire. Through Mira bringing tea and not commenting.

**What the reader should feel but never see:** Initiative. Action economy. The timing track. HP and wound slots. Armor values. Tier tables for healing. The stabilization roll. Persistent-wound penalties. Recovery intervals. All of it translated into sensation, consequence, and character.

## Tone

Tense, muscular, then tender. Opening act is methodical — the Society's competence laid bare in planning and preparation, the quiet before. Middle act is chaos: specific, granular, without romanticism. Combat as it is, not as it's pictured. Kael's revolver clicking on an empty chamber is a single small sound that should stop the reader. The final act is quiet — the slow work of recovery, the bonds named in gesture rather than declaration, and the shadow of consequences returning home with them.

Where Story 02 was a vast outward expedition (Wilds, awe) and Story 03 was a claustrophobic descent (tunnels, dread), Story 04 is open ground. Road, valley, sky. The openness is exposure, not safety. The forest gives cover. The Sootborn give sanctuary. The city they return to gives nothing.

## Structure

8 chapters, rotating tight third-person POV. Target 10,000–12,000 words. Chapters average 1,200–1,500 words.

POV rotation: Mira → Aldric → Kael → Sera → Aldric → Kael → Sera → Mira. Balanced 2/2/2/2.

## Chapter Plans

### Chapter 1 — Mira POV

**Setting:** The Wet Ember. Three days after the Story 03 coffee-house exchange with Fels. Late afternoon into evening.

**Scene:** Thessaly Vane comes to them. Again. Twice in one series of jobs now — patron-to-Society meetings don't usually go this direction. Mira logs the detail. The Covenant has read Mira's debrief on the Collective's expedition and its leadership is alarmed. The Collective succeeding at the mill — reaching the artifact, deploying industrial Galvanic suppression to breach the spatial defenses — is unacceptable. But the Covenant cannot be seen to oppose them directly. A patron-to-patron confrontation is political suicide; a Covenant-branded counter-expedition would be the same. What the Covenant needs is deniable action, and the Society is perfect: chartered, visible, but not officially aligned. Working-class operators the Covenant contracts on a job basis. A hit on the convoy that cannot be traced back to the Covenant.

The brief: delay or disable the convoy. Not destroy it — the Covenant does not want a body count that triggers Greycoat Authority intervention or civilian outcry. Damage the equipment. Scatter the contractors. Set the expedition back long enough that the Collective has to remobilize with replacement materiel — weeks, not days. The Covenant will pay well. The Covenant will also provide materiel: a cart for transport, blasting charges (traceable only to a Sootborn agricultural supplier the Covenant already sources from, i.e. deniable), communications gear, and access to Covenant intelligence about the convoy's composition and route.

The carrot that isn't actually a carrot: the Sootborn community at the Wild edge. Pell has been contacted by Covenant agents and has confirmed she wants the convoy stopped. Her people moved out there to escape the factions and have no interest in hosting a militarized push across their country. They won't fight. They will help — local knowledge, hidden trails, refuge. This is not an alliance. It is a convergence of interest. Mira registers the difference.

The team debate in the flat, later that evening. Kael is grimly practical. They're not soldiers, but they're closer to soldiers than most Societies, and if the Covenant is providing the equipment, they should take the job. The Collective reaching the artifact would be worse than this job's risks. Aldric already calculating his combat magic budget — this will be different from the tunnel expedition. Combat casting is a different economy: bigger casts under more pressure, with injury probability high enough that he needs healing reserves held back at all times. Sera is quiet. She agrees to the job. She doesn't volunteer reasons. Since the artifact, she has been quiet in a way no one has named. Mira decides not to name it yet either.

Mira's own calculation: this job takes them further from deniability than anything they've done. If it goes well, the Covenant will support them through anything that comes after. If it goes badly, they are very alone. She takes the job anyway.

**Preparation.** The second half of the chapter is the Society being excellent at what they are actually excellent at: preparing carefully. Kael oils his hunting rifle — long-barrel, bolt-action, scoped — for the first time since the Ashwick Job; he has been carrying the short revolver on jobs, but this job needs range work. He cleans the revolver anyway. He packs a backup blackpowder pistol for an ankle holster. Knife. Spare knife. Aldric packs his healer's kit — bandages, splints, clean water, the physical tools for when magic isn't enough. He double-checks his notebook is packed; he always writes after an event, and after this one he expects to have a lot to write. Sera adjusts her compass cord and tests the Aether around the flat — practice reaches, small Kinesis, reading her own state. She thinks she is at baseline. She isn't entirely sure. Mira packs the plan: a folded map, a Covenant seal for if they need to prove status, a pouch of Sootborn agricultural-grade blasting charges, a signal flare for abort, written schedules.

They leave Ashwick at dawn the next morning, the borrowed Covenant cart loaded with gear, and take the northeast road. The countryside is ordinary. The silence is ordinary. The silence has the specific quality of a room before someone says something difficult.

**Ends on:** Arrival at the Sootborn settlement, sundown of the second day. Pell at the edge of the road. She does not smile. She says: "Come inside. We have maps."

**Key details:**
- Thessaly coming to them twice — the power balance shifting
- The commission framed as deniable action — political constraints named
- Sootborn convergence-of-interest, not alliance — Mira noting the difference
- Covenant providing materiel — cart, charges, intel — the "we'll pay for the right tools" beat
- Four-position team debate: Kael practical, Aldric budgeting, Sera quiet, Mira deciding
- **Kael pulling the rifle** — establishing his range-work capability
- **Kael's backup pistol and the revolver both** — layered weapons for layered situations
- Sera's continued post-artifact quiet — carried over from Story 03
- Mira's calculation: the risk-to-deniability ratio
- The careful preparation as the Society's signature competence
- Pell's grim welcome — not hostility, not warmth, acknowledgment

### Chapter 2 — Aldric POV

**Setting:** Sootborn settlement common house, evening. Maps on the table — Pell's hand-drawn, annotated with trails, water crossings, sight lines. Rain against the shutters. Dawn the following day, the ridge positions overlooking the ford.

**Scene:** Aldric watches the plan form. He's been in planning sessions before; this one has a weight he can feel. Pell explains the land with the fluency of someone who walks it daily. There is a road — the Old Cart Road, built for timber haulage before the Tear, maintained since by the Sootborn and the last village — that cuts through a shallow valley between two wooded ridges. The Collective's convoy must use it to reach the mill: it's the only road wide enough for their equipment. The narrow point: a ford across a cold, stone-bottom river at the valley's floor. The road drops to the ford, crosses in shallows, climbs up the far side through a switchback. A convoy at the ford must string out. One vehicle at a time, or two abreast at most. That is the chokepoint.

The tactical geography: ridges on both sides offer fire positions; woods on the east ridge are thicker, offering better cover and a harder approach for pursuers. Sootborn trails, invisible to outsiders, let the Society come and go without touching the road. The Collective's Galvanic suppression gear — field dampeners, resonance disruptors, portable dead-zone generators — has limited range. If the Society is on the ridges, outside effective dampener range for the opening strike, Sera's casting stays viable through the critical window.

**Mira's three-phase plan.** She builds it out loud, the team contributing:

- **Phase 1 — Strike.** Simultaneous opening, timed tight. Kael on the east ridge with the rifle — take the lead vehicle's driver at ~210 yards. A beat later, Aldric casts Barrier at the far side of the ford — strong tier, the largest single cast of his life, designed to hold long enough to seal the convoy in the valley. Sera on the west ridge — Kinesis or Force on a prepared log cache, dropping it down the slope to block the rear. Convoy trapped: nose, tail, and cross-ridge fire.
- **Phase 2 — Sabotage.** Two-minute window. Kael descends from the east ridge, crosses the convoy floor, plants the Sootborn-sourced blasting charges on the convoy's equipment trailers (especially the Galvanic suppression hauler, the priority target). Aldric and Sera provide covering magic from the ridges — Shield and Kinesis as needed. Mira watches for an off-convoy pursuit contingent they all know Ironwork will have. Kael sets timers. Kael withdraws.
- **Phase 3 — Withdrawal.** Explosives go. Society melts back along Sootborn trails to a hidden clearing three miles southeast. Horses waiting — a Sootborn gift, to be returned. Ride west around the blocked convoy, avoid the road entirely, return to Ashwick via a southerly route.

**Aldric speaks his magical budget aloud so the team knows.** This is the Story 02/03 pattern — resource transparency. He opens with Barrier at strong tier, holds it for the first two minutes through Phase 1 into Phase 2. That cast is expensive. Strong tier is something he has practiced but has never deployed in anger; the cost curve at strong tier is punishing. If anything goes wrong with Phase 1, he's already into the red. After he releases the Barrier, he conserves — Shield in reserve for the inevitable injury, Mend held back for critical cases. He won't cast offensive magic during Phase 1. Sera is the offensive caster. Aldric is support, and support only. "My job is keeping us alive."

**Internal POV: the scholarly caster on combat casting.** In Story 03, his casting was meditative — the lattice built at leisure, the Aether invited into a frame. Combat is different. The lattice must form fast, faster than training calls for. The boundary between "structured discipline" and "panic pulling magic through a broken vessel" gets very thin under fire. He has studied this. He has practiced this. He has never done it for real. He acknowledges, privately, that he is frightened.

**Sera on the west ridge, the next morning.** Her internal monologue. The zone here is moderate Aetheric — the Wild edge leaking into the countryside — with flux pulses layered on top. She spent an hour on her ridge the previous evening reading the pulse pattern. It isn't predictive. It's perceptive — she can tell, in the moment, whether the Aether is thick or thin, surging or guttering. She works with what's there. She cannot plan around it.

**Kael with the rifle.** The shot. He's been practicing at range — not sniper-trained, but a good rifleman. Two hundred and ten yards, slight downhill, stable braced position, wind checked. He can make it. He thinks about the driver. He thinks about the man in the cab who is going to have a wife or a son or a mother. He thinks: that's the trade. He checks the wind again.

**Pell's offered contribution.** Three Sootborn will pose as travelers on the road ahead of the convoy at dawn, confirming composition and equipment one last time. Numbers match Fels's Story 03 intel. Three Ironwork pursuit vehicles expected. If anything has changed, the scouts will mark a marker point the Society will check on the way to position. Pell makes clear: reconnaissance only. Her people do not fight. She says it with the flat look Aldric remembers from Story 02.

**The signal flare.** Mira carries it. One flare = abort. Abort means: scatter, Sootborn trails, rendezvous at the hidden clearing. Do not try to salvage. Go home.

**Aldric at his ridge position, twelve hours before the strike.** He lays out his kit on a borrowed field desk. Bandages. Clean water in a flask. Splints. His notebook. The rituals of preparation. They do not calm him the way they normally do. He notes this. He keeps preparing anyway.

**Ends on:** Dawn. The convoy is expected mid-morning. Aldric on the east ridge with a small pair of Greycoat-issued binoculars. The ford empty. The water moving over stone. Then, very faint from the north: the sound of engines.

**Key details:**
- Pell as the ground expert — the Sootborn's terrain literacy
- The ford as the natural chokepoint
- Three-phase plan: strike, sabotage, withdrawal — with explicit contingencies
- **Aldric's strong-tier Barrier as the opening structural move** — biggest cast of his life
- **Aldric's magical budget spoken aloud** — continuing the team's transparency tradition
- Aldric's internal: the scholar-caster afraid for the first time
- **Sera's zone-flux reading** — the wild caster's combat adaptation
- Kael's rifleman mindset — the specific craft of the shot
- Pell's scouts confirming composition — intelligence-as-infrastructure
- The flare as abort signal — plans include their own failure conditions
- Horses waiting for withdrawal — Sootborn gift
- Aldric's unsettled preparation — the nerves real

### Chapter 3 — Kael POV

**Setting:** East ridge overlooking the ford. Clear morning. Slight breeze.

**Scene:** Kael in his nest. Rifle braced on a folded jacket over a log rest. The shot preparation described in its specific reality — not the cinematic version, the procedural one. Wind read. Range paced. Zero checked. Breath discipline. Heartbeat pause. He will take the shot between beats.

The convoy comes into view. Eight vehicles. Three heavy trucks (equipment — Galvanic suppression gear in the middle one, almost certainly). Two armored transports (Ironwork, ten to fifteen men). Two light utility vehicles (Collective researchers). One outrider motorcycle (scout). Moving slower than Kael's estimate — about 15 mph, the road rougher than the Sootborn map suggested.

The lead truck reaches the ford. The driver eases into the crossing. Kael takes the shot.

**The rifle kicks.** The driver's head snaps back; the truck rolls to a stop mid-ford, engine stalling in the water. Convoy behind it brakes. Horns. Voices. The plan unfolds.

Aldric's Barrier rises in Kael's peripheral — a shimmer of force across the far side of the ford, sealing the road. The convoy nose-locked.

Sera's log cache comes down — Kael hears it more than sees it, a rolling thunder of wood and stone crashing down the west slope. Rearward convoy blocked. Trapped.

**Phase 2.** Kael slings the rifle across his back, draws the revolver, takes the knife in his off-hand. Descending the ridge by the trail he and Mira cleared the night before. Two minutes on the clock.

The Ironwork contractors are reacting fast — trained muscle. They've piled out of the armored transports and taken cover behind vehicles, firing upward at the ridges. They don't have targets. But they are laying suppression fire. Kael hears rounds cracking through the trees above him.

He reaches the valley floor. Fifty yards of open ground between him and the convoy. He goes across it at a sprint, low and fast. A round cracks past his shoulder — someone saw him. He dives behind the second truck. Heart hammering. Breath ragged.

**The charges.** First charge on truck three — the Galvanic suppression hauler, priority target. Two-minute timer, set. He moves to truck two — munitions hauler, probably. Charge. Timer. He has three minutes of work and two minutes to do it in; he's going to be tight.

A contractor rounds the corner of the truck. The knife moves before Kael thinks — years of close-quarters drill from his Greycoat years. The contractor goes down. Kael doesn't look. He keeps moving.

Third charge. Fourth. One more.

A grenade bounces past him. He throws himself flat. The blast is close — maybe twenty feet. Shrapnel cracks into his forearm. He feels it, filing it, keeps moving.

**Final charge on truck four.** He turns to retreat — and a contractor is at the corner of the truck, pistol raised. Kael's revolver comes up —

*Click.*

The revolver misfires. Not a jam he can clear. Not a dud round he can recycle. Something in the mechanism — the accumulated Aether pressing against Galvanic engineering, the interference he felt in the tunnels now returned with a lethal edge — refusing to work.

The contractor fires. Kael feels something hit him — side, low, punching through his jacket and deep. He falls back against the truck. His legs give out. The pain is delayed — then it arrives, enormous.

He hears himself say "hell." He hears it because it is his voice, because that is what his mouth does when he has been shot. He has been shot before — once, as a Greycoat. It felt like this. A punch that keeps punching.

**He is on the ground behind the truck.** The contractor is advancing. Kael rolls sideways, draws the ankle-holster backup — the small blackpowder pistol, old but reliable, no complicated mechanism for the Aether to interfere with — and fires at the contractor's legs. The contractor screams and goes down.

Kael tries to stand. His legs do not work right. His vision is narrowing. He is losing blood faster than his body can adjust to. He begins to crawl — knife in one hand, backup pistol in the other — back toward the ridge trail. Boots leaving red streaks on gravel.

**The charges detonate.** The convoy erupts in cascading fire — Galvanic suppression first, then munitions, then the rest. The blast wave picks Kael up and throws him ten feet. He hits stone. He hears his own ribs crack. His vision whites out.

When he comes back, he is on his back, looking up at sky. His arm is wrong. His side is wet. He tries to breathe and coughs blood.

"Aldric," he says. Or thinks he says. "Mira." He cannot tell if the sound is leaving his mouth.

Something shifts in his chest — the deep wrongness of a lung filling with blood, though he does not know to name it that. The edges of his vision are going dark.

**Ends on:** Kael on his back, the sky above him, the fire and smoke of the burning convoy, the sound of gunfire receding, and a distant faint voice — Aldric's — calling his name.

**Key details:**
- Kael's rifle craft described procedurally — the specific reality of the shot
- Convoy composition documented — Galvanic gear, Ironwork, researchers
- Aldric's strong-tier Barrier executing
- Sera's log cache dropping
- **Kael shifting to knife at close range** — his identity under pressure
- **Revolver misfire at critical moment** — Story 03 seed paying off
- **Kael gets shot** — side entry, penetrating, critical-track injury
- **Backup pistol from ankle holster** — simple mechanism, reliable in zones where Galvanic engineering fails
- **Charges detonate, blast wave compounds injury** — broken ribs, possible lung damage
- Kael conscious and crawling — not a blackout, but an extended ebb
- The distant voice — Aldric calling his name, setting up Ch 5
- The death timer begins

### Chapter 4 — Sera POV

**Setting:** West ridge. Same morning. Before, during, and after Kael goes down.

**Scene:** Sera at the log cache position. She has been on the ridge for an hour before the strike, reading the Aether — not to predict it but to know what it is in this moment, and this moment, and this one. The flux is real and irregular. The pulses rise and fall without pattern she can see. She learns, over the hour, to feel each pulse as it arrives and cast what the Aether will bear, not what her training script says.

**Kael's shot is the signal.** She waits two seconds — the convoy needs to stop — then pulls Kinesis on the log cache. The Aether is thin on this pulse. The cast comes guttering. Weak tier — barely enough to tip the cache. The logs roll slowly. She curses silently, braces, pulls again — *Force* this time, harder shape, more aggressive draw — and the Aether surges just as she reaches. Strong tier. The logs avalanche properly, crashing down the slope in a rolling thunder, blocking the rear of the convoy in a cascade of wood and stone. But the strong cast has cost her — exhaustion already spiking. Two casts in five seconds. Not a pace she can sustain.

She takes a breath. Steadies. Shifts to her alternate position — a gap in the rocks with line-of-sight to the convoy floor. The plan: covering fire from the ridges. She watches the valley.

Contractors are firing upward at the ridges, blind suppression. They can't see the Society. But bullets impact the rocks around her position. She stays low.

**She sees Kael reach the convoy.** She sees him sprint across the open ground. She sees the contractor round the corner of the truck — and she casts before she thinks. Kinesis — a sharp kinetic push to knock the contractor off balance. The Aether surges on her reach. Strong tier. The contractor flies backward, slams into the truck behind him with force. Kael's knife finishes it. Sera loses another chunk of reserves.

She tracks Kael as he moves down the convoy, planting charges. She repositions along the ridge to keep him in sight. The contractors have realized there is a caster on the west ridge and are hunting for her now — rifles, a machine gun unlimbering on one of the armored transports. Rounds cracking into the rocks near her position. She keeps moving. Stays ahead.

**She sees Kael at the fourth truck.** She sees him plant the final charge. She sees the contractor appear at the corner of the truck. She sees Kael's revolver come up.

*Nothing happens.*

Her heart stops. She reaches for Kinesis — too late, too far, the contractor is already firing —

The shot lands. Kael goes down.

**Sera is screaming** — she hears herself — and the Aether is there, right *there*, a pulse cresting, the air alive with it. She reaches and pulls without shape or plan or control. The magic comes through her like floodwater through a collapsed dam. It has no structure. She has given it none. What passes through her is a pure reach with nothing on the other end of the line.

The ridge face she is on shudders. Dust rises in a cloud. Stones shift on the slope below. The Aether visibly luminescing in the air around her — blue-white light that Mira, fifty yards off at her own position, sees across the valley and will remember. The contractor advancing on Kael is lifted and thrown backward twenty yards. The truck beside Kael is pushed sideways on its wheels. Everything within thirty feet of Kael is cleared.

She does not know what spell that was. She did not shape it. The magic answered her panic with everything it had.

The backlash hits a heartbeat later. Caster's Mark: her eyes blazing bright enough to visibly light her face even in daylight. The exhaustion folds her — she drops to her knees on the rocks, hands shaking, the fog total. She has used strong-tier casts three times in ten minutes plus whatever the uncontrolled surge was. Her reserves are not low. Her reserves are *gone*.

**The charges detonate.** The convoy explodes in cascade — Galvanic suppression truck first, then munitions, then the rest. The blast rolls up the ridge in a wave of air and sound.

Through the smoke and fire, she sees Kael. Crumpled. A dark shape against gravel. Not moving.

Her body is a wreck. Her vision is narrowing. The ringing in her ears — the same ringing from Story 03 after the Barrier — is back and louder.

**But she has one more thing she can do.** The Collective researchers in the light utility vehicles are emerging from cover, trying to coordinate a response, calling orders. Three of them. Aldric needs a path to Kael. The researchers — non-combatants, disorganized, but still bodies on the field between Aldric and Kael — must be cleared or delayed.

She reaches one more time, through the fog, through the exhaustion, through the weariness that is physical now. Kinesis. Weakest tier she can aim. A directed shove at the researchers' feet — just enough to disrupt them.

The cast comes out weak. Three researchers stumble. They don't fall. It's enough. They scatter for cover rather than coordinate.

She calls across the valley: "GO!"

Aldric is already moving. She sees his distant figure leaping down the east ridge trail, across the ford, up the other bank.

Sera sinks to her knees on the rocks. The world pulses. Her vision narrows. The ringing in her ears deepens. She is not sure if she is going to stay conscious.

**Mira's voice beside her.** "Sera. Stay with me. Breathe." Mira has made her way across the ridge during the chaos — she doesn't remember Mira arriving. Mira's hand on her shoulder. Mira's other hand on her wrist, feeling for pulse.

"Alive?" Mira asks.

"Tired," Sera says. It's the best word she can find.

Mira nods. "Okay. Okay." She puts an arm under Sera's shoulders. "Stay with me. Aldric's got him. Stay with me."

**Ends on:** Sera slumped against Mira, watching from the ridge as Aldric's distant figure runs toward Kael through smoke. The Barrier across the ford still holding — still — behind him, like the last wall of the world.

**Key details:**
- **Zone flux reading under pressure** — Sera casting what the moment allows
- **Weak-then-Strong cast sequence** — wild caster variance made visceral under fire
- **Kinesis as covering fire** — combat caster's reality (force used offensively)
- **The uncontrolled surge when Kael falls** — pure emotional reach, no structure, magic answering panic with flood
- **Caster's Mark visible at distance** — Mira sees the glow from fifty yards
- **Exhaustion cascading** — three strong casts + surge = near-collapse
- **The final weak Kinesis she pushes through** — the weakest she can manage, and it's enough
- **Sera not knowing what she did** — the uncontrolled surge as mystery, not command
- Mira arriving on the ridge — the command role that includes caring for the wounded
- The Barrier still holding behind Aldric — the dropped-action moment already imminent

### Chapter 5 — Aldric POV

**Setting:** East ridge → valley floor → Kael's position on the convoy floor.

**Scene:** Aldric has been holding Barrier at strong tier for three minutes. His reserves are at roughly 40% and falling — the cost of sustained strong-tier casting is not a single expenditure but a drain, a tax on every breath he takes while the spell stands. He can feel Sera's uncontrolled surge from the other ridge — not because he saw it, but because the Aether around him rippled outward from a massive disturbance at her position. He doesn't know what she did. He knows it was too much.

Then he sees Kael fall.

Through his binoculars: Kael at the truck, the contractor firing, Kael going down. The shape of the wound from this distance. The body position of a man who has had something critical damaged inside him.

**The decision makes itself.** It is not a calculation. It is not a choice between tactical priorities. It is a reflex of the kind the scholar-path is not supposed to have. The Barrier is the structural centerpiece of the ambush — drop it and the far side of the road is back in play, which means the Collective can push through, which means the convoy can re-form, which means the job fails. Holding it is the rational act.

He drops it.

**The Barrier collapses.** He feels the cast release — the sudden absence of a structure he had been holding for three minutes, his hands and mind freed. The Aether rushes back into him chaotically as the spell unthreads, the unchanneled backflow of a major sustained cast terminating early. A wobble in his casting sense he will still be feeling days from now. But he cannot care about that now.

**He is running.** Down the ridge trail, faster than safety allows, scree sliding under his boots in places. Across the stream at the ford — cold water to his knees, does not slow him. Up the opposite bank. Across the convoy floor through smoke and burning vehicles and bodies and shell casings. Sera's second Kinesis has cleared the immediate area around Kael — he sees the researchers stumbling for cover off to his left. He ignores them.

Kael on the gravel. Pale. Blood pooling under him faster than it should. Breathing shallow. Cyanotic at the lips — oxygen already failing. The punch wound in the side. Aldric lifts the jacket. Exit wound at the rear, smaller — the bullet passed through. Kael's forearm is cut and bleeding from the grenade shrapnel. His arm lies at an angle that means something is broken. His ribs — Aldric's palm, pressed lightly to Kael's chest, feels the wrong softness of bone displaced by the blast wave.

**The time window.** Aldric estimates, forcibly, calmly, in the way the healer's training drilled: this is a through-and-through to the lower torso, placement indeterminate. If the bullet caught a major vessel, minutes. If it caught an organ (liver, kidney), fifteen minutes before the blood loss cascades to irreversible shock. If it only caught soft tissue and he can stop the bleeding, longer. He has to assume the worst case.

**The scholarly caster's method in extremis.** The lattice has to form fast — faster than training calls for. He builds the structure for Mend at strong tier. The Aether flux is helping now — pulses pushing into his frame, offering more than he has shaped. Offering the strongest tier, the spectacular tier, the cast that could seal Kael completely and restore his blood. He refuses. *He holds the boundary.* Spectacular would drain him to nothing and leave Kael needing further casts Aldric could not give. Strong is what he has reserves for. Strong is what he can cast twice. He holds the boundary.

**The cast comes at exactly strong tier.** His hands on Kael's side. The spell works inward. It is not closure — it is stabilization. The bleeding slows, does not stop. The organs — he can feel them now, through the cast's feedback, though that feeling is something he will not describe in his notebook later because words for it do not exist — begin to repair, slowly. The shock is moderated. Kael is still unconscious. The death timer is reset.

**Aldric's reserves are ~15%.** He has one more significant cast. It has to be the right one.

He looks up. The fire is spreading from the convoy. Wind pushing the smoke toward the ford. They need to move Kael now. He murmurs Shield on Kael at standard tier — just enough to keep the wounds from reopening during transport, and to pad the ribs from the jostling. The last real cast he has. He is running on the fumes. Like Sera two hours ago. Like Sera still.

**Kael's eyes flutter open.** Briefly. "Aldric," he rasps.

"I'm here," Aldric says. His voice is steady. His hands are shaking. "We're getting you out. Stay with me."

Kael's mouth moves. Words don't come. His eyes close again.

Aldric stays crouched there — shaking, depleted, watching Kael breathe — until Mira reaches him with Sera limping alongside, Sera's eyes still faintly glowing, face ashen.

"How bad?" Mira asks.

"Through and through," Aldric says. "I've stabilized him. But he's at the edge. We need shelter and I need time. Real time. Not this."

Mira nods. She is already planning the extraction.

**The stretcher carry.** Four hands on the stretcher handles — Aldric, Mira, and Sera, despite Sera being barely able to stand. They move Kael across the convoy floor. Through the fire. Back across the ford. Up the east ridge trail. Along it. Onto the Sootborn-marked withdrawal path.

The fire spreads behind them. The convoy is a pyre. Ironwork contractors — some retreating, some wounded and abandoned, some dead. No pursuit yet. Three miles to the rendezvous clearing and the horses.

**Ends on:** Aldric looking back once as they enter the forest trail. The ford behind them. The Barrier gone. The convoy burning. The road blocked nose and tail. The mission accomplished. The cost visible.

**Key details:**
- **Three minutes of strong-tier Barrier** — the drain beyond the initial cost
- **Dropping the Barrier as a non-choice** — the dropped action as reflex, not calculation
- **The Aether backflow when a sustained cast terminates** — the wobble that persists
- **Aldric running the terrain** — physical commitment matching the magical
- **The scholarly method under time pressure** — lattice forming fast, still holding the tier boundary
- **Tier discipline in extremis** — refusing Spectacular when the Aether offers it; choosing Strong twice over

- **Kael's brief consciousness** — "Aldric" / "I'm here"

### Chapter 6 — Kael POV

**Setting:** Forest trail through the east woods, Sootborn-marked. Afternoon into evening. Later: a Sootborn cabin room, firelight.

**Scene:** Kael's consciousness flickers like a lamp with bad wiring. This chapter is written in fragments — points of view that arrive without connective tissue, because that is what his mind is doing.

*Being carried.* Rough hands, known hands — Aldric's grip, Mira's grip. The motion of the stretcher. The smell of smoke still in everyone's clothes.

*Sera's face close to his.* Pale. Her eyes wrong. "Stay with us," she is saying. He wants to answer. His mouth will not.

*The forest canopy overhead.* Leaves against sky. He catalogues the trees from habit, as if he is scouting. Oak. Ash. More oak.

*Pain.* Distant, as if it belongs to someone else, but his, because it radiates from a place that is him.

*Water at his lips.* He cannot drink. Aldric's voice: "Small sips. Try, Kael. Try."

*The cart.* They have reached the rendezvous. Horses. He is being loaded onto the cart.

*Mira's voice somewhere above him:* "How far to the settlement?" A Sootborn voice answering: "Eight miles. By back trails. We go at dusk."

*The jostling.* Each bump a knife reopening.

**Between the fragments, interior.** Not structured flashback — the way a failing mind drifts. He thinks about the first man he ever shot, as a Greycoat, the day after his twenty-first birthday. He thinks about his father, who was not a good man but who taught him how to clean a rifle. He thinks about a girl named Teodora he knew before he joined up. He thinks about Mira's face across a table three years ago, telling him she was forming a Society and asking if he wanted in. He thinks *yes* was the easiest word he ever said.

He thinks: I don't want to die here.

He thinks: I'd rather die here with them than anywhere without them.

He thinks these things in the broken, not-quite-verbal way of a man on the edge. He does not say them. He does not need to.

**Aldric checking on him every hour.** Not real healing — Aldric is depleted. But fingers on his pulse. His pupils checked with a borrowed hand-lamp at dusk. Water pressed to his lips. "Come on, Kael. Stay with me. We're close."

**Dusk.** The cart stops. Voices. He is being lifted. Walked — no, carried — between two supports. Inside a building. Firelight. A bed with rough blankets.

Aldric's face close. "I'm going to do what I can. It's going to hurt." Kael cannot answer.

**The second Mend.** Aldric has recovered enough from forced rest to have something to give. The spell works deeper this time. The internal damage. The shredded tissue. The blood lost can't be replaced, but the torn places can be knit, the major vessels sealed properly, the ribs encouraged back into alignment. Kael feels heat along his side, bone-deep, as the magic works. It is not unpleasant — it is not pleasant either. It feels like his body arguing with itself and winning in a good way.

When it finishes, Kael is still pale, still weak, still wounded. But the bleeding is stopped. The organs are closing. He will live, if infection does not take him.

He opens his eyes properly. Sees Aldric above him, sweat on his brow, trembling.

"Thank you," Kael says. His voice is a rasp.

Aldric blinks. Then smiles — the small, contained smile of a man who does not smile often. "Anytime."

"Next time — your turn to get shot. I'll heal you."

Aldric's laugh is short. Dry. Tired. "Deal."

**Mira in the doorway. Sera behind her.** Both watching.

"Hi," Kael says to them.

"Hi," Sera says. Her eyes are wet.

Mira does not say anything. She nods. Kael sees what she is saying without words — *I was scared. I am glad you are here. I am not going to say I was scared.*

Kael closes his eyes. He sleeps for three days.

**Ends on:** Kael drifting off, the sound of the Sootborn settlement outside — a woman laughing about something, children running, the creak of a well crank, the low conversation of Mira and Aldric in the next room, Sera's quiet footsteps somewhere overhead. The ordinary sounds of people living. He sleeps.

**Key details:**
- **Wounded POV — fragmented consciousness** — combat cost rendered subjective
- **Being carried** — the man who always has his knife now being cargo
- **Aldric's second Mend at the settlement** — deeper cast, recovery begun
- **Kael's interior at the edge** — who he thinks about when he thinks he's dying
- **"Thank you" / "Anytime"** — the Aldric-Kael bond sealed in three words
- **"Next time your turn / Deal"** — dry, specific, the way men like them express love
- **Mira's unspoken relief** — the commander's feeling she does not name
- **Sera's wet eyes** — the wild caster's moment of uncomplicated relief
- **Three days of sleep** — deep recovery beat, time passing
- The ordinary sounds of the settlement — the world continuing around him

### Chapter 7 — Sera POV

**Setting:** Sootborn settlement. Days 3 through 8 after the strike.

**Scene:** Sera recovering. She is less physically wounded than Kael, but the magical depletion is deeper than anything she has known. Aldric checks her every day. Her pulse. Her pupils. The accumulation ring in her eyes.

**Aldric's honest assessment.** He tells her about the uncontrolled surge. The cast had no structure. What came through was massive — heavy kinetic flux plus a psychic component he cannot name without more study. The kind of cast that leaves changes. He tells her as a healer, not as a scholar: she needs to understand what her body *is* now, not what it used to be.

The accumulation ring is thicker. The iris discoloration is permanent, and has advanced. In routine light, it's visible without her needing to be under casting stress. Aldric does not describe this as progress or damage. He says: "It is change. You are changing. It is faster than anyone documented."

Sera asks him directly: "Is it bad?"

Aldric thinks for a long time. He says: "It is dangerous. Whether it is bad depends on what you become."

She considers it. She says: "Thank you for not lying."

**Pell visits.** She brings soup. She sits on the edge of Sera's bed — not close, not far. She tells Sera about Sootborn children who were born with markings. A boy with pale patches on his wrists. A girl whose hair grew in copper at the roots. They are not sick. They are not cursed. They are Wild-edge children. The Aether is their baseline. Pell does not know if Sera's changes are like theirs. She says: "You'll find out. The Wild is slow, but it tells the truth."

**Kael recovering alongside her.** Up by day five, moving slowly, one hand always near his side. He comes outside into the afternoon sun. He sits with Sera on a bench against the common house wall, both of them wrapped in blankets like invalids, which they are. They do not talk much. The talking they do is specific.

Sera: "Thank you for not dying."

Kael: "Thank you for not letting me."

They do not elaborate. They do not need to.

**Aldric writing.** The field notebook, the real one. Long hours at the table. He documents what it felt like to drop the Barrier — the Aether backflow, the persistent wobble in his casting sense (a subtle thing, like a bell that was rung too hard, still ringing days later at a frequency only he can hear). The strong-tier Mend in field conditions. The difference between stabilization and healing at the spell level. His resource curve: how far into the red he went, how long he stayed there, what recovery has looked like. The cautionary record. He will read it before the next job, and hope he remembers what it cost.

**Mira.** Quiet. Present. Checks on everyone daily but does not hover. She spends long hours with Pell — walking the settlement boundaries, listening more than she talks. Pell does not offer an alliance. She offers something softer: if the Society ever needs a place to run to, the settlement has cabins at the edge of the trees that no one will ask questions about. It is not membership. It is kinship. The kinship of people who stopped trusting factions. Mira accepts it with a nod and nothing more.

**The bonds, visible in small things.** Kael's first meal back at the common table — Aldric cutting his meat for him because Kael's arm is still slow. Sera resting her head against Kael's shoulder as they sit by the fire on the fifth evening. Mira bringing them tea and not commenting on any of it. Aldric and Mira planning together at the table, voices low, the conversation that happens between people who share command.

**Sera's interior movement.** The artifact vision is still in her — the network, the convergence. She has been carrying it alone since the tunnels. She thinks about telling Aldric. She thinks about telling Kael. She thinks about what they would do with the information. She decides, by the end of the sanctuary days, that she will tell them. Not yet. Soon. When they are all back to themselves. She does not know when that will be.

**She walks the edge of the settlement at dusk on day seven.** The Wilds visible past the trees. The Aether audible to her in a new way — quieter than in the city, steadier, less pulsing. The Wilds do not demand anything from her. They simply are.

She understands, standing there, that she is going to have to choose. At some point. Between being the wild caster who gets pulled deeper, and being a person who decides things. She does not know yet which she will be. She thinks about Pell's phrase: *whether it's bad depends on what you become.*

**Eight days.** Kael is walking without support. Aldric has recovered most of his reserves — the wobble persists but is fading. Sera is still in the fog but can cast small things without pain. Mira has the preparation for the return journey planned and provisioned. They are ready. Or close to ready.

**Ends on:** Morning of departure. The cart is loaded. Pell at the edge of the road. Mira and Pell exchange a look — not a farewell, not a contract, just acknowledgment. Pell says: "Stay alive." Mira says: "You too." The Society rides south-west, back toward Ashwick. Sera looks back once at the settlement disappearing behind the trees. Then forward, toward home.

**Key details:**
- **Physical and magical recovery on different timelines** — eight days is enough for partial, not full
- **Aldric's assessment to Sera** — "change is dangerous. Whether it's bad depends on what you become"
- **Pell's Sootborn children** — the Wild-edge kids whose Aetheric exposure shaped them without breaking them
- **Kael-Sera bench scene** — the unspoken thank-yous
- **Aldric's field documentation** — the scholarly cautionary record of his own resource failure
- **Mira-Pell quiet kinship** — people who left factions recognizing each other
- **Small domestic bonded moments** — meat cut, head on shoulder, tea without comment
- **Sera's decision to share the vision eventually** — the secret softening
- **Sera at the settlement edge** — the thematic framing for her arc ("what you become")
- Return begins — the home they are returning to is not the home they left

### Chapter 8 — Mira POV

**Setting:** Return journey → the Wet Ember → the flat on Harrier Street. Late afternoon into evening of return day.

**Scene:** The return is quiet. The cart moves south through ordinary countryside that feels unreal after the Wild edge. They talk more than on the way out. Kael rides in the cart, not driving, Sera beside him. Aldric and Mira on horses. Sootborn-gift horses, to be returned later by a party they will send once things have settled. The phrase *once things have settled* keeps occurring to Mira in a way she does not like.

Ashwick comes into view at sunset. Smoke from the factories still burning coal, the western sky stained. The gradient visible from this angle as a subtle haze where the two zones meet. Mira feels something — she has been away from the city for twelve days — the way you feel when you walk into a house you know well and something is slightly off and you cannot yet name it.

**Thessaly at the Wet Ember.** She comes to them. Again. Mira logs that detail. The Covenant is treating them carefully.

Thessaly's debrief: the convoy action succeeded as specified. Three trucks destroyed, Galvanic suppression gear crippled, six Ironwork contractors dead, four wounded. Collective has officially postponed the expedition. Intelligence suggests approximately two months before they can remobilize with replacement equipment. The Covenant is pleased. Payment is transferred at a premium rate.

**Thessaly's warning, private.** She takes Mira aside at the bar. Her voice is quiet, her expression composed. "They know it was you. They don't have evidence. But they know. I can't protect you from what comes next. I can give you information, support, materiel. I cannot put the Covenant's name between you and them."

Mira: "What comes next?"

Thessaly: "Agents. Assassins. Observers. Probably some combination. Be careful where you sleep. Be careful who you trust. The Collective plays for keeps when they have been hurt." Pause. "I'm sorry."

Mira nods. Does not respond. The transaction is complete.

**She does not relay the warning to the team when they leave the Wet Ember.** She tells them the mission is accepted as complete. She tells them the Covenant is pleased. She tells them the Collective is remobilizing but the expedition is delayed. She does not tell them about the warning. She decides — without fully articulating the decision to herself — that tonight they need to rest, that Kael in particular needs one night of feeling safe, and that tomorrow is soon enough to redistribute the weight.

Kael watches her deliver the summary. He does not call the edit. But she sees him noticing. The pattern is now named between them, and he is watching for it. The fact that she is doing it again, on the same day, is information she cannot retract. She accepts that.

**They go to Harrier Street.** The flat is as they left it. Almost.

Mira notices, in sequence:

- **The lock on the front door.** Same lock. A faint scratch around the keyway — the kind of scratch a skilled but imperfect pick leaves. She opens the door normally, notes the detail, does not raise it.
- **Kael's revolver.** They locked it in the kitchen drawer before leaving (he took the rifle and the backup). The drawer is closed exactly as she remembers. But when she opens it to put away a small parcel, the revolver has been moved — perhaps half an inch from its original position. Someone has opened the drawer and examined it. She does not touch the revolver. She closes the drawer.
- **The back-alley window.** Always latched. Always with the catch fully engaged. The catch is engaged now at a slight angle, as if it was unlatched and re-latched by someone who doesn't know exactly how she does it.
- **Mrs. Brant across the landing.** She brings over tea — genuine welcome, the widow neighbor whose company Mira finds gentle — and mentions that two men came looking about four days ago. Said they were from the Greycoat Authority, doing a welfare check. Mrs. Brant thought it was odd (Greycoats do not do welfare checks in this neighborhood) but she told them the flat's occupants had been gone for a week going on two. The men thanked her and left. She describes them without realizing what she is describing. Mira listens and smiles and sips her tea and mentally catalogues every detail Mrs. Brant provides.

**Mira files all of it.** She does not tell the team. Not yet. Tonight they need to rest. Kael is home and safe-feeling and recovering. Let him have the night.

**The team settles.** Aldric at the kitchen table with his notebook. Sera at her window seat with her journal. Kael on the couch with a book, the low lamp on, one hand periodically moving to his side. The flat feels like home. The team feels like family.

Mira sits by the front window as evening fully falls. She looks out at the street. Harrier Street quiet. The gas lamp at the corner casting its familiar yellow-orange pool. Across the street, in the alley opposite, there is a shape that could be a man standing in shadow, or could be a trash receptacle, or could be nothing.

She watches.

After ten minutes, she still cannot tell. The shape has not moved.

She does not say anything. She sits down in her chair and opens her own journal. The lamplight is warm. The tea is good. The team is home.

**The note she takes,** in her shorthand, reads: *Possible surveillance, west alley. Lock scratches. Window catch. Drawer disturbed. Collective response initiating. Watchful. Not tonight.*

**The final image.** Sera's compass — returned to its place on the coat hook — spins once in the still air, gently. Then settles. The needle wavers. Points true.

For now.

**Ends on:** The compass settling. The pen scratching. The lamp burning. The shape in the alley not moving.

**Key details:**
- **Thessaly's private warning** — Mira's information advantage
- **Mira not telling the team tonight** — the continuing pattern, consciously chosen, Kael watching
- **The flat's subtle discontinuities** — lock, drawer, window, neighbor's story. Each small, together unmistakable.
- **Mrs. Brant as accidental informant** — ordinary life revealing the hunt
- **The shape in the alley** — ambiguous. Surveillance vs. paranoia. The reader sees what Mira sees, no more.
- **Mira's journal entry** — her command private record, the discipline of filing without acting
- **Home as shadow-haunted** — the domestic tableau with a watcher outside
- **The compass callback** — echoing Story 03's closing image, extending the series pattern of stories ending on a still, ominous beat
- **No cliffhanger, no action** — the weight is in the unspoken, the shadow, the filed-but-not-raised
