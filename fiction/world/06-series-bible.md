# Series Bible

## Ash & Veil Recovery — Story Continuity

This document tracks continuity across the interconnected short stories. It should be updated after each story is written.

---

## Series Overview

A series of interconnected short stories following Ash & Veil Recovery, a small Adventuring Society in the fractured city of Ashwick. Each story is self-contained -- a complete arc with its own stakes and resolution -- but threads connect across stories, building toward larger tensions and deeper character development.

The stories showcase different facets of the Aetherfall world: urban investigation in the fractured city, expeditions into Wild Zone territory, encounters with Galvanic technology, the moral complexity of faction politics, and the personal cost of living between two forces that refuse to share.

## Story List

### Story 1: "The Ashwick Job" (Prequel / Backstory)

**Status:** Exists as game module. Not written as fiction.
**Summary:** Ash & Veil's first job. Inspector Laine hires the newly chartered Society to find Fen Harker, a missing Gradient Scholar researcher. The trail leads through Ashwick's three zones -- a neutral-ground tavern, a Galvanic foundry, and Aetheric tunnels beneath it -- revealing a Lamplighter Syndicate smuggling operation moving charged Aetheric artifacts.
**Outcome:** Harker rescued. Syndicate operation disrupted. Society's charter validated.
**Seeds planted:** The anomalous Aetheric readings beneath the foundry. The tunnels go deeper than anyone has mapped. The Syndicate knows the Society's faces.

### Story 2: "What the Mill Remembered — A Tale of the Ash & Veil Society"

**Status:** Complete. Published at `/fiction/what-the-mill-remembered.html`.
**Summary:** The Covenant of Embers sends Ash & Veil on their first patron-directed job: investigate rumors of an artifact discovered by Sootborn explorers inside a pre-Tear mill ruin in the Wilds northeast of Ashwick. The Society drives out through ordinary countryside, abandons the car at the last Wild-edge village, finds a Sootborn settlement that refuses to help, pieces together enough information to navigate the Wilds, and reaches the site: the shell of an old grain mill whose interior has been remade from the inside out by the Aether into something that was never human. The artifact -- a sealed container with no maker's mark -- is visible in a chamber within but inaccessible behind architectural defenses no one can breach. The return becomes a survival fight when Ironwork Solutions tries to eliminate them in the Wilds.

**Key events:**
- Thessaly Vane delivers the briefing at the Wet Ember, visibly hurried -- a race against the Collective
- The drive northeast: progressive vehicle unreliability approaching the Wild edge; quarantine markers from failed post-Tear government cordons; the last village with its yard of abandoned cars
- The Sootborn settlement refuses to guide them; Sera feels kinship with the Sootborn
- Aldric pieces together intelligence from fragments: Thom's account of Old Pell's grandfather's mill, a child's two-layer drawing (mill silhouette below, alien geometry above), the explorers who came back changed and confused about time
- The Wilds: spatial distortion, landmarks that move, Sera navigating by Aetheric feel, Kael's revolver going sluggish, Aldric's spells coming in too hot
- The mill interior: industrial bones completely replaced with pale seamless material, luminous veins, warm surfaces, corridors that shift. Both Aetheric and Engine-like properties in the same substance
- Kael's forearm cut when a wall settles mid-marking; Aldric's Mend comes in too eager
- The container visible but unreachable -- spatial defenses loop any approach back to the corridor
- A structural shift traps them; Sera casts Anchor to stabilize the atrium; Kael leads the retreat through rearranging corridors
- Sera's first Force cast that hurts someone badly -- visible emotional cost
- Running fight with Ironwork in the Wilds: zone shifts mid-combat, guns and magic flickering in and out; they escape using Wilds navigation, not combat victory
- Pell reveals the mill was her grandfather's -- family ties to the site; Sootborn hostility shifts to grim understanding
- Dace Rennick waiting at a crossroads on the return -- the Margin was watching; Mira gives him the story without the meaning
- The deadlock established: known site, inaccessible artifact, multiple factions aware

**Outcome:** Site confirmed but artifact inaccessible. Ironwork Solutions revealed as willing to use lethal force outside city limits. The Collective knows about the site. The Margin knows the site is real. Deadlock: everyone wants what's inside the mill and nobody can get it.

### Story 3: "The Lamplighter's Price" (Planned)

**Status:** Plan complete. Not yet written.
**Premise:** Corrigan Fels blackmails the Society into retrieving lost Syndicate cargo from the deep tunnels beneath the Ashworth Foundry. What they find -- the same transformative agency from the mill, operating beneath a Galvanic factory -- reframes the world's central mystery. Thematic focus: magic as lived experience (wild casting vs. scholarly casting showcased through Sera and Aldric).

## Locations

### The Wet Ember
- **Type:** Tavern, neutral ground
- **Location:** Ashwick, near the gradient border
- **Details:** Gas lamps, coal fire, worn wood bar. Hesper Brack behind the bar. Factory workers from the west, Quarter residents crossing for the beer. The Society's unofficial meeting point.

### The Flat on Harrier Street
- **Type:** Residence, Society headquarters
- **Location:** Ashwick, Harrier Street (gradient border)
- **Details:** Small, functional. Kitchen table doubles as workspace. The Society's home. East faces the Veilwright Quarter (copper air, luminescence), west faces the factories (hum, arc lights).

### The Sootborn Settlement (Wild Edge)
- **Type:** Small community at the forest edge
- **Location:** Northeast of Ashwick, at the Wild boundary
- **Details:** Cluster of buildings from salvaged materials and local timber. Gardens that grow too well. Sweet-tasting well water. ~2 years old. Accessible by road to the last village, then on foot.

### The Last Village
- **Type:** Rural settlement at the Wild frontier
- **Location:** Northeast road, last stop before the Wilds
- **Details:** Six houses, shared well, ward-post at its boundary. Man in leather apron runs a yard of abandoned cars -- decades of travelers who left vehicles and didn't come back. Charges a mark a week.

### The Mill (Wilds)
- **Type:** Pre-Tear industrial ruin, Aetherically transformed
- **Location:** Wilds northeast of Ashwick, maybe an hour's walk from the settlement (but time/distance lies in the Wilds)
- **Details:** Shell of Old Pell's grandfather's grain mill -- two silos, loading bay, smokestack stub. Interior completely remade: pale seamless material, luminous veins, warm surfaces, shifting corridors. Both Aetheric and Engine-like properties. A sealed container visible in a central chamber, unreachable behind spatial defenses. The structure is alive -- walls settle, corridors shift, geometry rearranges.
- **Status:** Deadlocked. Artifact visible but inaccessible. Collective planning armed expedition with Galvanic suppression equipment.

### Ashworth Foundry & Tunnels
- **Type:** Decommissioned Galvanic factory with deep tunnel system
- **Location:** Ashwick, Factory Quarter (moderate Galvanic territory)
- **Details:** Dark windows, cold smokestacks, chained loading bay. Storm drain access on the west side. Tunnel system: old drainage infrastructure being transformed by the same agency as the mill. Aetheric staircase gradient (sharp zone transitions). Deep tunnels nearly fully transformed -- pale material replacing brickwork, luminous veins, warm surfaces. The transformation predates the Syndicate and possibly the Tear.
- **First explored:** The Ashwick Job. **Revisited:** Story 03.

## Future Story Concepts

These are seeds, not outlines. Each should be developed into a full premise when the time comes.

**"Down the Gradient"** -- A job takes the Society deep into the Veilwright Quarter for the first time. They encounter the ritual circle (the senior casters maintaining the zone) and must decide what to do with the knowledge. Showcases the Quarter as a living community, not just a zone on a map.

**"Iron and Embers"** -- The Covenant asks the Society to retrieve a prototype Galvanic device -- not from an enemy, but from a researcher who's gone rogue and is experimenting with magitech hybrids in an abandoned gradient zone. The patron's Awakening alignment makes the assignment uncomfortable: why does the Covenant want Galvanic technology?

**"Lean Season"** -- The Covenant's funding is delayed. The Ledger drifts toward lean. The Society takes a Greycoat Authority job that nobody else wants -- not because it's dangerous, but because the pay is bad and the work is unglamorous. A routine zone survey in a residential neighborhood reveals that the Ashworth Collective's expansion is further along than anyone realized, and the people living in the affected blocks are running out of time.

**Combat-focused story (TBD)** -- Earmarked as the combat showcase: Kael-heavy, damage, timing track, armor, Galvanic devices, weapon reliability. The Collective's armed expedition to the mill could be the trigger -- the Society racing to intercept or counter a militarized retrieval with industrial-grade Galvanic suppression equipment.

**"The Deep Wild"** -- Dr. Crane hires the Society for the job she's been building toward: an expedition into the Deep Wild to find the missing research team. The Society leaves Ashwick entirely, entering territory where the rules of reality are suggestions. Sera's connection to the Aether deepens. Kael's distrust of magic is tested by an environment where his gun is useless and the only thing standing between him and the Wild is the casters he doesn't fully trust.

**"The Split"** -- The Ironveil Circle's theological divide becomes public. Engine Orthodox and Veil Contemplatives break into open faction warfare, dragging their respective Societies into the fight. The Circle's neutral sanctuaries -- hospitals, orphanages -- are caught in the middle. Ash & Veil is drawn in through their connection to the Quarter and the gradient communities that depend on the Circle's services.

## Continuity Rules

1. **Injuries persist.** If someone gets hurt in Story 2, they're still healing in Story 3 unless enough time has passed.
2. **Relationships develop.** Interactions in one story affect dynamics in the next. If Kael and Sera have a breakthrough moment, reference it.
3. **The world moves.** Faction dynamics shift between stories. Zone boundaries creep. NPCs pursue their agendas whether the Society is watching or not.
4. **Seeds grow.** Every planted detail should eventually pay off or be explicitly abandoned. Don't leave threads dangling without purpose.
5. **Time passes.** Stories don't happen back-to-back. Days, weeks, sometimes months between jobs. The Society has a life between the stories -- acknowledge it through incidental details (a new dent in Kael's jacket, Sera's hair grown out, Aldric having finished a book he was reading).
6. **Magic costs accumulate.** Sera's accumulation progresses across stories. Backlash events leave residue. The physical marks of casting are continuity details.
7. **Each story showcases a game facet.** Story 02: the world and zones. Story 03: magic systems. Future: combat, economy, faction politics. The mechanics are invisible to the reader but vivid to anyone who reads the rulebook.
