# Series Bible

## Ash & Veil Recovery — Story Continuity

This document tracks continuity across the interconnected short stories. It should be updated after each story is written.

---

## Series Overview

A series of interconnected short stories following Ash & Veil Recovery, a small Adventuring Society in the fractured city of Ashwick. Each story is self-contained -- a complete arc with its own stakes and resolution -- but threads connect across stories, building toward larger tensions and deeper character development.

The stories showcase different facets of the Aetherfall world: urban investigation in the fractured city, expeditions into Wild Zone territory, encounters with Galvanic technology, the moral complexity of faction politics, and the personal cost of living between two forces that refuse to share.

---

## Story List

### Story 1: "The Ashwick Job" (Prequel / Backstory)

**Status:** Exists as game module. Not written as fiction.
**Summary:** Ash & Veil's first job. Inspector Laine hires the newly chartered Society to find Fen Harker, a missing Gradient Scholar researcher. The trail leads through Ashwick's three zones -- a neutral-ground tavern, a Galvanic foundry, and Aetheric tunnels beneath it -- revealing a Lamplighter Syndicate smuggling operation moving charged Aetheric artifacts.
**Outcome:** Harker rescued. Syndicate operation disrupted. Society's charter validated.
**Seeds planted:** The anomalous Aetheric readings beneath the foundry. The tunnels go deeper than anyone has mapped. The Syndicate knows the Society's faces.

### Story 2: (First written story -- premise below)

**Working title:** TBD
**Premise:** The Covenant of Embers assigns Ash & Veil their first patron-directed job: recover an Aetheric artifact from a location outside Ashwick proper -- a site in the gradient borderlands where a pre-Tear structure has been exposed by recent zone shifts. The artifact is a sealed container of unknown origin that the Covenant believes holds a preserved specimen of condensed Aether predating the Tear -- evidence that the Veil was thinning long before it ruptured.

The job is straightforward until it isn't. Ironwork Solutions is also hunting the artifact, on assignment from the Ashworth Collective. The Margin shows up because Dace Rennick heard about it through his network and wants a finder's fee from whoever gets it first. A Sootborn community near the site is nervous about outsiders digging in the gradient and may help or hinder depending on how the Society approaches them.

**Why this story works as the first:**
- Introduces the Covenant of Embers as patron (Thessaly Vane delivering the briefing)
- Introduces both rival Societies in action (Ironwork as opponents, the Margin as wild cards)
- Takes the story outside Ashwick into the borderlands -- showing the world beyond the city
- Creates a moral dilemma: the artifact's historical significance vs. the risk of multiple factions fighting over it, vs. the Sootborn community's legitimate concerns about zone disruption
- Showcases all four characters in their roles: Kael handling the Ironwork confrontation, Sera navigating strong Aetheric territory, Aldric analyzing the artifact, Mira negotiating with the Sootborn and possibly with Dace
- Demonstrates the zone gradient in a non-urban setting -- the borderlands feel different from Ashwick's streets
- Plants seeds: what does a pre-Tear Aetheric artifact imply about the timeline everyone believes? If the Veil was thinning before the Tear, what does that mean for the three competing theories?

## Future Story Concepts

These are seeds, not outlines. Each should be developed into a full premise when the time comes.

**"Down the Gradient"** — A job takes the Society deep into the Veilwright Quarter for the first time. They encounter the ritual circle (the senior casters maintaining the zone) and must decide what to do with the knowledge. Showcases the Quarter as a living community, not just a zone on a map.

**"Iron and Embers"** — The Covenant asks the Society to retrieve a prototype Galvanic device -- not from an enemy, but from a researcher who's gone rogue and is experimenting with magitech hybrids in an abandoned gradient zone. The patron's Awakening alignment makes the assignment uncomfortable: why does the Covenant want Galvanic technology?

**"The Lamplighter's Price"** — Corrigan Fels calls in his debt. The Syndicate needs something recovered from a place the Society has already been -- the deep tunnels beneath the Ashworth Foundry. The price of refusal is information about the Society reaching the wrong ears. The price of acceptance is doing a job for people they don't trust.

**"Lean Season"** — The Covenant's funding is delayed. The Ledger drifts toward lean. The Society takes a Greycoat Authority job that nobody else wants -- not because it's dangerous, but because the pay is bad and the work is unglamorous. A routine zone survey in a residential neighborhood reveals that the Ashworth Collective's expansion is further along than anyone realized, and the people living in the affected blocks are running out of time.

**"The Deep Wild"** — Dr. Crane hires the Society for the job she's been building toward: an expedition into the Deep Wild to find the missing research team. The Society leaves Ashwick entirely, entering territory where the rules of reality are suggestions. Sera's connection to the Aether deepens. Kael's distrust of magic is tested by an environment where his gun is useless and the only thing standing between him and the Wild is the casters he doesn't fully trust.

**"The Split"** — The Ironveil Circle's theological divide becomes public. Engine Orthodox and Veil Contemplatives break into open faction warfare, dragging their respective Societies into the fight. The Circle's neutral sanctuaries -- hospitals, orphanages -- are caught in the middle. Ash & Veil is drawn in through their connection to the Quarter and the gradient communities that depend on the Circle's services.

## Continuity Rules

1. **Injuries persist.** If someone gets hurt in Story 2, they're still healing in Story 3 unless enough time has passed.
2. **Relationships develop.** Interactions in one story affect dynamics in the next. If Kael and Sera have a breakthrough moment, reference it.
3. **The world moves.** Faction dynamics shift between stories. Zone boundaries creep. NPCs pursue their agendas whether the Society is watching or not.
4. **Seeds grow.** Every planted detail should eventually pay off or be explicitly abandoned. Don't leave threads dangling without purpose.
5. **Time passes.** Stories don't happen back-to-back. Days, weeks, sometimes months between jobs. The Society has a life between the stories -- acknowledge it through incidental details (a new dent in Kael's jacket, Sera's hair grown out, Aldric having finished a book he was reading).
