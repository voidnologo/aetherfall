# The Society & Other Parties

## Ash & Veil Recovery

---

## The Society

**Name:** Ash & Veil Recovery
**Charter:** Asset Recovery -- retrieval of artifacts, specimens, people, and information from contested, hazardous, or zone-compromised territory.
**Size:** Four active members.
**Age:** Newly chartered. Their first completed job was the Ashwick Foundry operation -- the rescue of Fen Harker from a Lamplighter Syndicate smuggling tunnel. They are not famous. They are not established. They are hungry, principled, and learning fast.

**Reputation:** Minimal, but growing. Inspector Laine of the Greycoat Authority considers them competent. The Gradient Scholars know them through Aldric Wynn's former employment and the Harker rescue. The Lamplighter Syndicate knows them as the people who disrupted one of their operations, which is both a credential and a liability. Most of Ashwick has never heard of them.

**Operating Philosophy:** The Society takes jobs that put them in places where most people won't go -- Aetheric zones, Galvanic dead zones, gradient borders, and the contested spaces between factions. They recover things: artifacts from Wild Zone ruins, prototypes from rival installations, missing people from bad situations. "Recovery" is a flexible word. It covers theft, rescue, retrieval, and occasionally things that blur the line between all three.

They are not mercenaries. They have a line. They don't kill people who've surrendered. They don't destroy things that don't need destroying. They don't take jobs that require them to hurt civilians, even when the pay is good. This limits their earning potential and makes them less attractive to patrons who need deniable violence. It also makes them the kind of Society that people trust -- provisionally, carefully, but genuinely.

**Base of Operations:** A rented flat above a closed cobbler's shop on the east end of Harrier Street, near the Aetheric side of the gradient. The location was chosen by Mira, who grew up on this street and knows that the gradient here gives them advance warning of zone shifts in either direction. The flat is cramped: a main room that serves as office, meeting space, and argument venue, two bedrooms (Kael and Aldric share; Sera and Mira share), a kitchen that's mostly used for coffee and first aid, and a closet that Aldric has converted into a library of exactly eleven books.

## The Patron: The Covenant of Embers

**Type:** Private foundation, Awakening-aligned.
**Focus:** Aetheric artifact preservation, research, and -- when necessary -- containment.
**Meta-faction lean:** Awakening, moderate. They believe Aetheric artifacts are expressions of something genuine and important about the world post-Tear, and that understanding them is both a moral obligation and a strategic necessity. They do not worship the Aether. They study it, protect it, and make sure it doesn't fall into the hands of people who would weaponize it or destroy it.

**Origin:** The Covenant was founded roughly fifteen years ago by a group of scholars, collectors, and former Adventuring Society members who noticed a pattern: Aetheric artifacts were being destroyed by Restorationist factions, hoarded by the Lamplighter Syndicate, and ignored by academic institutions that didn't have the funding to deal with them. The founders believed that these artifacts represented irreplaceable knowledge about the Aether, the Veil, and the changed world -- and that someone needed to recover and preserve them before they were lost forever.

**What they do:** The Covenant operates a small network of vaults across multiple cities, storing recovered artifacts in zone-controlled environments that prevent degradation or uncontrolled resonance. They fund research into artifact function and origin. They maintain relationships with the Gradient Scholars (academic allies), various Adventuring Societies (operational assets), and a web of informants who track artifact movement on the black market and in faction circles.

**What makes them interesting as a patron:** The Covenant's Awakening lean creates tension when they encounter Galvanic technology. They don't fear it -- they're too pragmatic for reflexive ideology -- but they're deeply curious about it, and their curiosity sometimes leads them to assign jobs that their Societies don't expect. A mission to recover an Aetheric artifact from a contested zone is standard. A mission to retrieve a prototype Galvanic device from an Ashworth facility because the Covenant wants to understand how it interacts with their artifact collection -- that's where the tension lives. What does it mean when your Awakening-aligned patron takes an interest in the other side's tools? Are they studying the enemy, hedging their bets, or seeing something that transcends the ideological divide?

**What they're not:** Corrupt. Fanatical. Secretly evil. The Covenant is a genuine organization with genuine beliefs, run by people who mean well and usually act accordingly. Their tension with the Society comes not from hidden agendas but from the natural friction between a patron that deals in knowledge and a Society that deals in action. The Covenant sometimes asks for things without fully explaining why. They sometimes prioritize artifact preservation over human safety, not out of callousness but out of a different calculation about what matters in the long term. They are good people who occasionally ask the Society to make hard choices, and they are honest about the fact that those choices are hard.

### The Handler: Thessaly Vane

The Society's point of contact with the Covenant. Not a field operative -- she works from the Covenant's regional office, a set of rooms above an antiquarian bookshop in Ashwick Central. She's the one who delivers assignments, provides resources, and serves as the institutional voice when the Society has questions.

**Age:** Late thirties.
**Appearance:** Tall, angular, dark hair worn short in a practical cut. Dresses in the conservative professional style of someone who spends most of their time in offices and libraries -- waistcoats, pressed shirts, no jewelry except a signet ring from an institution she doesn't discuss. Walks with a slight limp from a leg injury that healed wrong.
**Background:** Former Gradient Scholar who left academia after a field expedition went bad -- the details are unclear, but the limp dates from then. She joined the Covenant because it let her stay connected to the work without going back into the field. She's well-read, well-connected, and well-aware that her job is to balance the Covenant's interests against the Society's survival. She likes the Ash & Veil team. She will not risk the Covenant's mission for their comfort.
**Personality:** Dry, precise, occasionally warm when she forgets to maintain professional distance. She communicates through carefully written briefing packets that read like academic papers -- thorough, meticulously cited, and occasionally missing the point of what the Society actually needs to know for survival. Kael finds her infuriating. Aldric finds her familiar. Mira reads between the lines of her briefings with the same skill she reads people. Sera doesn't trust anyone who won't say what they feel out loud.

**Relationship with the Society:** Professional, warming toward personal. Thessaly assigned Ash & Veil their charter on the strength of Aldric's academic credentials and Mira's zone navigation experience. The Harker rescue -- a Greycoat Authority job, not a Covenant one -- impressed her because the Society took initiative, made good decisions under pressure, and didn't lose anyone. She is beginning to trust them, which means she is beginning to give them harder assignments.

## Rival Societies

### Ironwork Solutions

**Patron:** Ashworth Foundry Collective (Restoration)
**Archetype:** Asset Recovery / Zone Clearance
**Size:** Six members, well-equipped.

The Collective's premier Adventuring Society. Better funded, better armed, and better connected than Ash & Veil in every measurable way. They carry Galvanic weapons -- arc pistols, resonance grenades, field dampeners -- and they use them with the confidence of people who know their patron will replace anything that breaks.

Ironwork doesn't do subtlety. They walk into situations heavy, announce their authority (such as it is), and expect compliance. This works in Galvanic zones, where their gear dominates. It works less well in Aetheric territory, where their Galvanic loadout makes them visible, vulnerable, and resented.

**Leader: Captain Lena Moritz.** Former military officer, forties, steel-grey hair cropped close. She runs Ironwork with military discipline and military values: chain of command, mission first, acceptable losses. She considers Ash & Veil amateurs -- too small, too soft, too sentimental about collateral effects. She is not a villain. She's a professional who genuinely believes in the Restoration cause and genuinely believes that her patron's expansion is good for Ashwick. She is also wrong about some things, and she doesn't know it yet.

**Relationship with Ash & Veil:** Rivalry, shading toward contempt. Ironwork and Ash & Veil have overlapping operational territory -- both do asset recovery, and artifacts don't care who finds them first. Moritz considers the Covenant of Embers a sentimental organization that hoards artifacts that should be studied by proper (Restoration-aligned) institutions. She has not yet had reason to view Ash & Veil as a serious competitor, but the first time they reach an artifact before Ironwork does, that will change.

### The Margin

**Patron:** No formal patron. Arrangement-aligned, self-funded through job fees and side work.
**Archetype:** Fixers / Border Runners
**Size:** Five members, rotating.

A looser, more pragmatic outfit than either Ash & Veil or Ironwork. The Margin does everything: smuggling, escort, investigation, artifact recovery, information brokerage, and "conflict resolution" that occasionally involves violence but more often involves knowing who to bribe and how much. They're not chartered by a faction -- they work for anyone who pays, which makes them versatile, unpredictable, and morally flexible.

**Leader: Dace Rennick.** Early fifties, heavy-set, affable, perpetually smoking a thin cigar that he never seems to finish. Dace has been working the Ashwick gradient for twenty years. He knows everyone, owes everyone, and is owed by everyone. He's charming in a way that makes you wonder what he's not telling you -- the answer is always "something." He's not malicious. He's just a man who's learned that information is worth more than ideology, and he trades it accordingly.

**Relationship with Ash & Veil:** Cautious mutual respect, verging on friendship in certain lights. Dace likes the Ash & Veil team -- he finds their principles entertaining and occasionally admirable. He's the kind of ally who will help you when it serves his interests and apologize sincerely when it doesn't. The Margin and Ash & Veil have cooperated once (a gradient crossing that neither could manage alone) and competed once (a recovery job where the Margin got there first). The score is even. It won't stay that way.

## Other Key Relationships

### The Lamplighter Syndicate

Not allies. Not enemies. Complicated.

The Ashwick Foundry operation put Ash & Veil on the Syndicate's radar. Depending on how that job ended -- negotiation, capture, or chaos -- the Syndicate's attitude ranges from grudging respect to active wariness. Either way, the Syndicate knows the Society's faces, and in the Syndicate's world, knowing someone's face is the beginning of a relationship whether you want one or not.

**Corrigan Fels** is the Syndicate fixer most likely to cross paths with the Society. He's professional about it. He doesn't hold grudges about the foundry job -- that's business, and business sometimes goes wrong. He does, however, keep track of debts, and the Society disrupting a Syndicate operation is a debit in his ledger. He'll collect. Probably not with violence. Probably with a favor requested at the worst possible moment.

The Syndicate is a recurring presence, not a recurring enemy. They move through the same spaces as the Society -- the gradient, the neutral zone, the tunnels beneath Ashwick. They will be obstacles, information sources, reluctant allies, and occasional antagonists, sometimes in the same story.

### The Greycoat Authority

The Society's first job came from the Greycoats, and Inspector Laine remains their most reliable contact in official Ashwick. The relationship is transactional: Laine has problems and no staff; the Society needs work and credibility. Both sides benefit.

The tension is that the Authority is compromised -- Dupont's deals with the Collective and the Syndicate are eroding the institution's integrity, and Laine is starting to notice. The Society may eventually be caught between their loyalty to Laine (who is honest) and the Authority she represents (which isn't entirely). This is a slow burn, not an immediate crisis.

### The Gradient Scholars

Aldric's former employer and the source of the Harker connection. The Scholars view Ash & Veil with the fondness that academics reserve for former students who went into the field: slightly condescending, genuinely proud, and occasionally useful. Dr. Crane knows the Society exists and files the information for later -- she has research teams that need escorts, artifacts that need recovery, and a missing Deep Wild expedition that haunts her sleep.

The Scholars and the Covenant of Embers are natural allies -- both Awakening-leaning, both interested in understanding rather than exploitation. The relationship is not formal, but the overlap in interests means that assignments from the Covenant sometimes dovetail with Scholars' research, giving the Society two reasons to be in the same dangerous place.

### The Veilwright Quarter

The Society operates from the Aetheric side of Harrier Street, which makes them neighbors to the Quarter even if they're not residents. Maren Elia knows of them -- healers keep track of who's operating near the zone border, because operations near the border tend to produce casualties. The Quarter's attitude toward Ash & Veil is cautiously positive: they're Awakening-patron, they don't work for the Collective, and they didn't destroy anything during the foundry job. That's enough for provisional welcome, not enough for trust.

The ritual circle -- the senior casters maintaining the zone's strength -- is a secret that the Society doesn't know about yet. When they discover it, the question of whether to expose it, support it, or ignore it will be one of the hardest decisions they face.

## The Society's Place in Ashwick

Ash & Veil Recovery is one of perhaps a dozen active Adventuring Societies operating in Ashwick. They are not the biggest, the best-equipped, the most experienced, or the most connected. What they are is principled, adaptable, and willing to go places that more established outfits consider beneath them or too dangerous for the pay.

Their niche is the gap between factions. The jobs that don't fit cleanly into one patron's agenda. The artifacts that are valuable to the Covenant but coveted by the Collective. The missing people who disappeared in the gradient. The anomalies that the Scholars want studied but can't reach because their campus is on the wrong side of a zone shift.

They are a small Society in a small city doing work that matters locally and resonates nowhere else. Their names don't appear in broadsheets. Their jobs don't change the world. But they change Ashwick, one recovery at a time, and the people they help remember.

That's enough. For now.
