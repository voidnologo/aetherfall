# Starter Adventure: The Ashwick Job

**Type:** One-session quickstart module (3-4 hours)
**Players:** 4 (pre-generated characters provided)
**Format:** Shadowrun-style module — GM plot overview, scene-based structure with read-aloud "Tell It To Them Straight" blocks, decision branches, NPC stats, and pre-gen character sheets.
**Purpose:** Get a new table playing Aetherfall in one session. Showcase core mechanics without overwhelming with edge cases. Give players enough experience to make informed character creation choices afterward.

---

## 1. GM Overview

### The Plot

A newly chartered Adventuring Society takes their first job from the Greycoat Authority: find Fen Harker, a Gradient Scholar researcher who went missing three days ago in the Ashwick district. Harker was mapping zone drift patterns along the border between the Galvanic industrial quarter and the Aetheric old town. His last check-in mentioned "anomalous readings" near a decommissioned resonance foundry.

The truth: Harker stumbled onto a Lamplighter Syndicate operation smuggling charged Aetheric artifacts through a tunnel network beneath the foundry. The Syndicate grabbed him to keep him quiet while they finish moving the shipment. Harker is alive — the Syndicate doesn't want the heat of a dead Scholar — but he's running out of time. The shipment moves tonight, and once it's gone, Harker becomes a loose end.

### The Arc

The adventure moves the party through three distinct environments, each showcasing different mechanics:

| Act | Zone | Location | Primary Mechanics |
|-----|------|----------|-------------------|
| **Act 1: The Lead** | Neutral | The Wet Ember (tavern) + Harker's boarding house | Social encounters, skill checks, non-skill ability checks, investigation |
| **Act 2: The Foundry** | Galvanic | Ashworth decommissioned resonance foundry | Gun combat, timing track, equipment, Galvanic zone effects |
| **Act 3: The Tunnels** | Aetheric | Smuggling tunnels beneath the foundry | Magic casting, Aetheric zone effects, non-combat magic/tech utility, final confrontation |

### Tone

Noir investigation that escalates into action. The party starts asking questions in a smoky tavern, follows clues into an industrial ruin, and descends into tunnels where the Aether hums in the walls. The mood shifts from urban noir to industrial tension to something stranger and more wondrous underground.

### What This Module Showcases

**Explicitly demonstrated:**
- d100 roll-under skill checks (social, physical, investigative)
- Non-skill ability checks (raw attribute rolls)
- Combat on the timing track (declaration order, casting times, weapon speeds)
- Firearms (revolvers, a Galvanic arc pistol)
- Melee weapons
- Magic casting (at least one scholarly and one wild cast)
- Tier results and margin of success
- Aetheric/Galvanic zone effects on equipment and casting
- Wound penalties (death spiral — at least one NPC should get wounded and visibly degrade)
- Non-combat magical utility (Reveal for investigation, Mend for healing)
- Non-combat Galvanic utility (resonance detector, arc lamp)

**Intentionally deferred (too much for session one):**
- Backlash / Wild Effects (can happen naturally but don't force it)
- Enchanting, artifacts, attunement
- Spell learning, XP, progression
- Push It mechanic
- Exhaustion overflow
- Adventuring Society patron tension

### Pacing Guide

| Segment | Target Time | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Introduction & character handout | 10-15 min | GM reads the cold open, players review pre-gens |
| Act 1: The Lead | 45-60 min | Investigation, social encounters, 2-3 skill checks per player |
| Act 2: The Foundry | 45-60 min | Infiltration or assault, one full combat encounter |
| Act 3: The Tunnels | 45-60 min | Exploration, utility magic/tech, final confrontation, rescue |
| Wrap-up | 10-15 min | Resolution, hooks for continued play |

## 3. Cold Open

*Read this to the players after they've had a few minutes to look at their character sheets.*

### Tell It To Them Straight

> The Wet Ember smells like pipe smoke and old wood. It's a tavern on the neutral side of Ashwick — the kind of place where the Galvanic hum from the factory quarter and the faint Aetheric prickle from the old town cancel each other out. Neutral ground. The lamps are gas, not electric, not magical. The beer is warm. The fire is real.
>
> You're sitting at a corner table — the four of you, your Society charter still fresh enough that the ink smudges if you rub it. One week chartered. Zero jobs completed. The Greycoat Authority posted the contract yesterday: missing person, academic, last seen in the Ashwick border district. Pay is 200 marks split however you like, plus expenses. Not glamorous. But it's your first job, and first jobs matter.
>
> The door opens. A woman in a grey coat — actual Greycoat, not just the color — crosses the room and sits down across from you without asking. She's mid-forties, sharp eyes, ink stains on her fingers. She sets a thin folder on the table.
>
> "You're the new Society. I'm Inspector Laine. Let's skip the part where you try to impress me."

## 4. Act 1: The Lead

### Scene Goals
- Introduce the mission and key NPCs
- Let every player make at least one skill check
- Establish the three-zone geography of Ashwick
- Give the party 2-3 leads that converge on the foundry

### Location: The Wet Ember

**Mood:** Warm, dim, crowded enough for privacy. A neutral-ground tavern where factory workers and old-town residents drink side by side. Not friendly, exactly, but civil. The kind of place where people mind their own business and expect you to do the same.

**NPC: Inspector Renna Laine (Greycoat Authority)**

The party's handler for this job. Pragmatic, overworked, mildly skeptical of a brand-new Society but willing to give them a shot because she doesn't have the bodies to investigate this herself.

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| BR | 0 |
| PC | 0 |
| IN | +2 |
| SP | +1 |
| AW | +2 |
| PW | -1 |

**Key Skills:** Investigation 58%, Observation 58%, Persuasion 54%, Streetwise 54%, Firearms 50%
**Equipment:** Service revolver, badge, notebook, cynicism

**What Laine Knows:**
- Fen Harker, Gradient Scholar researcher, went missing 3 days ago
- He was mapping zone drift patterns along the Ashwick border — routine academic work
- His last check-in (via dead-drop note to the Scholars) mentioned "anomalous readings near the old Ashworth foundry"
- The foundry has been decommissioned for 2 years — officially empty
- Laine suspects squatters or smugglers but can't spare constables to check
- She hands over Harker's boarding house address and his last note

**What Laine Doesn't Know:**
- The Lamplighter Syndicate connection
- That the foundry tunnels exist
- That Harker is alive

### The Briefing

#### Tell It To Them Straight

> Laine opens the folder. Inside: a sketch of a thin man with round spectacles and a distracted expression, a hand-drawn map of the Ashwick district with zone boundaries marked in colored ink, and a note on university letterhead.
>
> "Fen Harker. Gradient Scholar. Forty-three years old, nearsighted, terrible card player. He's been in Ashwick for two months measuring zone drift — where the Aetheric and Galvanic boundaries shift over time. Boring work, but someone has to do it. Three days ago he stopped checking in. His people at the Gradient Scholars filed with us yesterday."
>
> She taps the note. "This is his last dead-drop. Read it."
>
> The note, in cramped handwriting: *"Anomalous readings near the decommissioned Ashworth foundry. Drift pattern inconsistent with natural decay — something is actively pulling the boundary. Investigating tomorrow. Will report findings at next check-in."*
>
> Laine looks at you. "He didn't report. His room's paid through the month. His equipment is gone. Either he found something interesting and lost track of time — which is possible, I'm told academics do that — or he found something dangerous. Find him. Bring him back. Try not to start a war with anyone."

**GM Notes — Mechanics to Showcase Here:**
- If a player asks Laine questions, this is a good moment for a **Persuasion** check to get extra info (she knows local Lamplighter activity has been higher than usual but won't volunteer it without trust)
- **Investigation** or **Observation** check on Harker's note: the handwriting gets shakier toward the end. He was nervous when he wrote this. The ink is smudged — he was writing fast.

### Location: Harker's Boarding House

**Mood:** Quiet residential street on the neutral side. The boarding house is clean but cramped — academics don't get luxury budgets. Harker's room is on the second floor. The landlady, Mrs. Olt, is worried but trying not to show it.

#### Tell It To Them Straight

> The boarding house is three stories of grey stone on a street quiet enough to hear the factory hum from two blocks west. A brass plaque reads "Olt's — Rooms by the Week." The landlady answers on the second knock. She's sixty-odd, flour on her apron, reading glasses pushed up into her hair.
>
> "You're here about Mr. Harker? Thank goodness. Come in, come in. His room's upstairs. I haven't touched anything — seemed wrong to, with him not back yet."

**NPC: Mrs. Olt (Landlady)**

Not a combatant or a faction operative. Just a worried landlady. She likes Harker — he's quiet, polite, pays on time. She'll answer any question honestly.

**What Mrs. Olt Knows:**
- Harker left three mornings ago with his equipment bag (heavy, clanking — survey instruments)
- He said he'd be back by evening
- He'd been going out to the foundry area regularly for the past two weeks
- She saw a man watching the boarding house yesterday — "rough-looking, long coat, smoking by the lamp post for an hour." He left when she stared at him from the window.
- She's never seen the man before

**Searching Harker's Room:**

This is the investigation showcase. Let the players describe what they're looking for and call for appropriate checks.

| Check | Target | What They Find |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| **Investigation** (IN-based, 50+) | Standard success | Harker's personal journal on the desk. Recent entries describe zone readings near the foundry. The last entry mentions "sub-basement access — ventilation grate on the east wall. Readings are strongest below ground." |
| **Observation** (AW-based, 50+) | Standard success | Boot prints in the dust under the window — someone climbed up from outside and searched the room before the party arrived. They were careful but missed the journal. |
| **Occult Lore** (IN-based) | Any success | Harker's survey instruments are missing, but his calibration notes are here. The readings he recorded show Aetheric intensity *increasing* near the foundry sub-basement — unusual, because the foundry is in the Galvanic quarter. Something Aetheric is down there that shouldn't be. |
| **Streetwise** (IN-based) | Standard success | The man Mrs. Olt described — long coat, smoking, watching the house — sounds like a Lamplighter lookout. The Syndicate uses that pattern: one watcher, rotating shifts, always near a lamppost (ironic, given their name). |

**GM Notes — Non-Skill Ability Check:**
If a player wants to force Harker's locked desk drawer, call for a **raw BR check** — roll d100 under (BR + 4) x 10. This is a good moment to demonstrate that you don't need a specific skill for everything; sometimes you just need to be strong enough.

Alternatively, Mira can pick the lock with **Sleight of Hand** — a skill-based approach to the same problem. Let the table see that different characters solve problems differently.

### Decision Point: Following the Leads

By this point the party should have 2-3 leads pointing to the foundry:
1. Harker's journal mentions the foundry sub-basement
2. The Lamplighter lookout suggests organized interest
3. Harker's readings show anomalous Aetheric activity underground

**Branch A — Go straight to the foundry.** Proceed to Act 2. The party arrives during daylight. The foundry exterior is guarded by two Lamplighter thugs posing as demolition workers.

**Branch B — Investigate the Lamplighter connection first.** A **Streetwise** check at The Wet Ember or on the street can turn up a local informant who confirms the Syndicate has been moving cargo through the area for the past month. "Something big, heavy, and makes your teeth itch if you stand too close." This adds context but ultimately still leads to the foundry. Adds 15-20 minutes.

**Branch C — Report back to Laine.** Cautious play. Laine thanks them for the intel but says she still can't spare constables. "That's why I hired you. The contract stands — find Harker." The party goes to the foundry anyway, but Laine provides one additional resource: a Greycoat signal whistle that will bring constables within 15 minutes if blown within the neutral zone. (It won't work inside the Galvanic or Aetheric zones — nice mechanical flavor.)

## 5. Act 2: The Foundry

### Scene Goals
- First combat encounter on the timing track
- Demonstrate firearms, melee, and weapon speed differences
- Show Galvanic zone effects (guns reliable, magic weakened)
- Demonstrate wound penalties (death spiral) on NPCs
- Create a clear transition into the Aetheric tunnels below

### Location: The Ashworth Decommissioned Resonance Foundry

**Mood:** Industrial ruin. The foundry is a hulking brick-and-iron structure on the edge of the Galvanic quarter. Smokestacks cold, windows dark, loading bay doors chained shut — but the chains are new. The Galvanic hum is stronger here. Sera (the wild caster) can feel it pressing against her — like trying to breathe through wet cloth. Aldric notices his spellbook pages feel heavier, sluggish.

**Zone Level: Galvanic +3 (moderate)**
- Firearms: Reliability normal (no penalty)
- Galvanic weapons: +6% to effective operation (+2 per net Galvanic level)
- Casting: -6% to casting target (-2 per net Galvanic level). Sera's target drops from 56 to 50. Aldric's from 50 to 44. Casting is harder here — not impossible, but the Aether feels distant.

#### Tell It To Them Straight

> The foundry hunches against the skyline like something that died standing up. Three stories of soot-stained brick, riveted iron beams, smokestacks that haven't breathed in two years. The loading bay faces the street — its doors are chained, but the chain is bright steel. New. Someone's maintaining this place.
>
> A sign on the fence reads "ASHWORTH INDUSTRIAL — DECOMMISSIONED — NO TRESPASSING — GREYCOAT AUTHORITY." Beneath it, someone has scratched in chalk: "NOT EMPTY."
>
> Two men in work clothes are sitting on crates near the loading bay, eating lunch. They look like demolition workers. They don't look like they want company.

**NPCs: Lamplighter Guards (2)**

Low-level Syndicate muscle. Their job is to keep people away from the foundry and report anything unusual. They're not killers — they'll fight if cornered but prefer to bluff or intimidate first.

### Approaching the Foundry

**Branch A — Talk your way in.** Mira's strength. A **Persuasion** or **Deception** check against the guards. Possible covers: Greycoat inspection (Mira has forged credentials), demolition assessment, lost and looking for directions. On success, the guards let them look around the ground floor — but get nervous if the party heads toward the stairs down. On failure, the guards tell them to leave and get visibly tense.

**Branch B — Sneak in.** Find another entrance. An **Observation** check reveals a broken window on the second floor, reachable by climbing a drainpipe (**Athletics** check, BR-based). Inside, the second floor is empty but footprints in the dust lead to a stairwell going down.

**Branch C — Confront directly.** Announce who they are, demand entry. The guards refuse and things escalate to combat. This is the fastest path to a fight.

### Combat Encounter: The Loading Bay

*This encounter triggers if the social approach fails, if the party is discovered sneaking, or if they choose direct confrontation. It can also trigger when the guards realize the party is heading for the sub-basement.*

#### Tell It To Them Straight (if combat starts)

> The pretense drops like a mask. The nearest guard kicks his crate aside and reaches for the revolver under his jacket. The other one is already moving, putting a stack of iron pipes between himself and you. The foundry floor is cluttered — old machinery, rusted conveyor frames, stacks of salvage. Plenty of cover. Plenty of things to trip over.
>
> Everyone acts. Declare what you're doing.

**GM Notes — Running the Fight:**

This is probably the table's first Aetherfall combat. Walk them through it step by step.

**1. Declaration order:** Lowest PC declares first (tells the table what they're doing before faster characters commit). Kael (PC +2) and Mira (PC +1) declare last — they see what others do.

**2. Timing track:** Set up a simple count track (1-10). Everyone places tokens at count 0. Each action has a speed:
- Light weapons (knife): speed 2
- Medium weapons (revolver): speed 4
- Casting a spell: varies by spell (Force is speed 3)

**3. Walk through one full exchange:**
- Sera declares Force at Guard A. Speed 3 — token at count 3.
- Guard A fires revolver at closest target. Speed 4 — token at count 4.
- Guard B draws knife and charges Kael. Speed 2 — token at count 2.
- Aldric holds back (no offensive spells — can prepare to Mend if someone gets hurt).
- Mira fires arc pistol at Guard B. Speed 4 — token at count 4.
- Kael fires revolver at Guard A. Speed 4 — token at count 4.

**Count 2:** Guard B reaches Kael with the knife. Roll attack. If hit, apply damage and Soak. If the damage pushes Kael into a wound tier, describe the penalty kicking in — he's Harmed now, -10% to everything. (This probably won't happen at 8 HP/tier with a 1d4+1 knife, but demonstrate the concept.)

**Count 3:** Sera's Force resolves. She rolls 2d100-take-farther against her casting target (50 in this Galvanic zone, down from 56). Walk the table through the margin → tier result. Remind them she can suppress 1 tier if she wants. Apply damage. Describe the Aetheric accumulation shifting the zone slightly.

**Count 4:** Three tokens resolve simultaneously. Kael fires, Mira fires, Guard A fires. Resolve each. The arc pistol shifts zone balance Galvanic by 2. Revolvers don't shift anything.

**Key teaching moments during combat:**
- **Declaration order matters:** Kael and Mira (high PC) see what everyone else does before committing
- **Speed matters:** The knife is fast (speed 2) but weak. The revolver is slower (speed 4) but hits harder. Casting has its own timing.
- **Zone effects are tangible:** Sera *feels* the Galvanic pressure on her casting. Call it out.
- **Wound penalties cascade:** When a guard takes a solid hit, describe them flinching, slowing — the -10% penalty isn't abstract, it's the death spiral in action.

**After the fight:**
The guards go down or surrender. They're not loyal enough to die for the Syndicate. If interrogated (Persuasion or Intimidation), they reveal:
- They work for the Lamplighter Syndicate
- There's cargo in the sub-basement — "heavy crates, weird glow, gives you a headache"
- A man matching Harker's description was brought in two nights ago — "the boss said hold him till the shipment moves tonight"
- There are 3 more Syndicate members below, including the operation lead, a woman named Corva
- The sub-basement connects to old tunnels that go under the zone border into Aetheric territory

### The Stairwell Down

#### Tell It To Them Straight

> The stairwell spirals down into darkness. The air changes immediately — warmer, thicker, with a faint smell like ozone and old copper. The Galvanic hum from the factory above fades with each step. Something else rises to replace it — a low, resonant pulse that Sera recognizes immediately.
>
> The Aether. Strong. Getting stronger.
>
> Aldric's spellbook feels lighter in his satchel. His fingertips tingle. The casting headache he's had since entering the Galvanic quarter eases, then vanishes. For the first time in an hour, the magic is close again.
>
> Mira checks her arc pistol. The charge indicator is flickering. Down here, the rules are about to change.

## 6. Act 3: The Tunnels

### Scene Goals
- Demonstrate Aetheric zone effects (magic enhanced, firearms/tech degraded)
- Showcase non-combat magical and Galvanic utility
- Final confrontation with Syndicate operation lead
- Rescue Harker
- Provide a satisfying resolution

### Location: The Smuggling Tunnels

**Mood:** Strange and wondrous. These aren't just tunnels — the Aether has been leaking through here for months, warping the environment. The walls shimmer faintly. Crystalline growths cluster in corners, glowing pale blue. The air tastes metallic and sweet. It's beautiful and deeply unsettling.

**Zone Level: Aetheric +4 (strong)**
- Casting: +8% to casting target (+2 per net Aetheric level). Sera's target jumps to 64. Aldric's to 58. Magic comes easy here — almost too easy.
- Firearms: Reliability drops. Revolvers still function (mechanical, simple) but jam on a roll of 85+ instead of the usual 96+. Mira's arc pistol is worse — Galvanic devices are actively suppressed. Reliability drops to 75+.
- Galvanic equipment: Flickering, unreliable. The arc pistol's charge indicator is erratic. Resonance devices buzz with static.

#### Tell It To Them Straight

> The tunnels open up. What was once a brick-lined maintenance corridor is now something else entirely. The walls are slick with condensation that glows faintly blue in the dark. Crystalline formations — thin as fingers, sharp as glass — grow from the ceiling in clusters. They pulse with a soft light, like a heartbeat.
>
> The air is alive. Sera can feel it on her skin — the Aether, thick and close, practically begging to be shaped. This is a Wild Zone, or close to one. The kind of place where magic doesn't just work — it sings.
>
> Ahead, the tunnel branches. Left curves downward. Right opens into something wider — you can hear voices, and the scrape of heavy objects on stone.

### Exploration Phase

This section is less structured than Act 2 — let the party explore, use their skills, and engage with the environment. The key moments:

**Non-combat magic showcase — Reveal:**
Aldric can cast Reveal to scout ahead. In this Aetheric zone, his target is 58 — comfortable odds. A Standard result shows him the layout: the right tunnel leads to a large chamber where three people are loading crates onto a handcart. One figure sits against the far wall, hands bound — Harker. A Weak result gives a vaguer sense — "people ahead, one of them isn't moving freely." Either way, it demonstrates divination as a tactical tool.

**Non-combat Galvanic utility:**
If Mira or Kael are carrying any Galvanic detection equipment (Laine could have provided a resonance detector as part of the job), it's going haywire — but usefully so. The readings confirm massive Aetheric artifact concentration in the chamber ahead. The crates the Syndicate is moving are charged artifacts. This detector also demonstrates that Galvanic tech has utility beyond weapons — it's a tool for understanding the environment.

**Environmental detail — crystalline growths:**
If anyone examines the crystals, an **Occult Lore** check reveals they're condensed Aether — raw magical energy crystallized over months of exposure. They're fragile, worthless commercially, but striking evidence that the Aether has been bleeding through here for a long time. This is what Harker was tracking — the zone drift that shouldn't exist.

**Ability check showcase:**
The left tunnel has a partially collapsed section. Getting through requires either:
- A **raw BR check** to shift rubble (non-skill ability check)
- A **Kinesis** spell from Sera to move the debris (magic solving a physical problem)
- Ignoring the left tunnel entirely (it's a shortcut to flank the chamber, but not required)

Smart, calm, professional. She runs smuggling operations, not a gang. She doesn't want to fight — violence brings Greycoat attention, which is bad for business. But she won't let the party take Harker or the cargo without negotiation.

Same statline as the guards above. One has a revolver, one has a shotgun (Medium speed 5, 3d4, devastating at close range but slow).

#### Tell It To Them Straight

> The chamber is larger than you expected — an old cistern or storm drain, maybe thirty feet across, the ceiling vaulted in crumbling brick. The Aetheric crystals are thickest here, throwing pale blue light across everything. It's almost beautiful.
>
> Three people are working around a stack of wooden crates. Two of them are muscle — same type as the guards upstairs. The third is a woman in a dark coat that looks too expensive for a smuggling tunnel. She sees you and doesn't reach for a weapon. She just stops. Looks at you. Tilts her head like she's calculating something.
>
> Against the far wall, a thin man with broken spectacles sits with his hands tied in front of him. Fen Harker. Alive. He sees you and his eyes go wide, but he doesn't speak.
>
> The woman in the coat folds her arms. "Well. That was faster than I expected. Let's talk about how this ends without anyone getting hurt."

### Decision Point: The Standoff

**Branch A — Negotiate.** Corva's preferred outcome. She'll offer a deal: the party takes Harker and walks away. The cargo disappears tonight. Everyone pretends this never happened. She'll even pay them — 100 marks, on top of whatever the Greycoats are paying. A **Persuasion** or **Intimidation** check can improve the terms (she releases the cargo manifest so the Greycoats know what's being moved, or she gives up her local contact network). This is the quietest resolution and showcases social mechanics in a high-stakes setting.

**Branch B — Demand everything.** Harker AND the cargo AND Corva's surrender. Corva refuses — the Syndicate doesn't accept total losses. Combat starts. This is a harder fight than Act 2: three enemies in a confined space with cover (the crates), and the Aetheric zone means Sera is supercharged while the party's guns are less reliable.

**Branch C — Create a diversion.** Sera or Aldric can use magic to create chaos — Force to scatter the crates, Kinesis to rip weapons away, Reveal to blind with light. In this Aetheric zone, even Weak tier spells come easy. The workers panic (they're not soldiers), and the party can grab Harker in the confusion. Corva escapes — she's too smart to fight a losing battle. This showcases magic as a tactical tool beyond damage.

### Combat Notes (if it happens)

**Zone effects in action:**
- Sera's casting target is 64 in this zone. She's rolling with confidence. Walk the table through how much the zone matters — compare to the foundry above where she was struggling at 50.
- Mira's arc pistol reliability is degraded. If she fires and rolls 75+, the shot fizzles — the Galvanic charge can't stabilize in the Aetheric field. She still has her derringer (mechanical, no zone penalty) as backup.
- Standard revolvers work but jam on 85+. Not likely, but the threat is there.
- If Sera casts Force, the Aetheric accumulation pushes the zone even further — making subsequent gun rolls even riskier. The tug of war is visible and consequential.

**Mend showcase:**
If anyone gets hurt, Aldric can cast Mend. In this zone his target is 58 — good odds. Walk through the casting time on the timing track (speed varies by spell), the tier result, and the healing. Demonstrate that Mend can change the outcome of a fight by getting someone out of a wound penalty tier.

### Rescue and Resolution

#### Tell It To Them Straight (after the confrontation resolves)

> Fen Harker's hands shake as you cut the rope. He flexes his fingers, winces, and looks up at you with an expression that's equal parts relief and academic excitement.
>
> "Thank you. Genuinely. I was beginning to worry they'd—" He pauses. Pushes his broken spectacles up his nose. Looks at the crates. "Do you know what those are? Those are charged Aetheric artifacts. Someone has been harvesting condensed Aether from these tunnels and packaging it for transport. The zone drift I was tracking — it wasn't natural. They've been *mining* the Aether. Pulling it through. That's why the readings were wrong."
>
> He stands, unsteady. Looks around the crystal-lit chamber with something like wonder.
>
> "This is the biggest find of my career and I discovered it by getting kidnapped. The Gradient Scholars are going to have questions. The Greycoats are going to have questions. Everyone is going to have questions."
>
> He looks at you. "I imagine you'll be the ones answering most of them."

## 7. Wrap-Up

### Returning to Inspector Laine

The party delivers Harker (and optionally the cargo manifest or Corva in custody). Laine pays the contracted 200 marks. She's impressed — quietly, professionally impressed.

**If they negotiated with Corva:** Laine frowns but accepts it. "You got the Scholar back alive. That was the job. But the Syndicate is still operating, and now they know we're looking. This isn't over." (Hook for future sessions.)

**If they took down the operation:** Laine is genuinely pleased. "The cargo is evidence. Corva's in custody. The Scholar is safe. Not bad for your first job." She takes the party's charter paperwork and stamps it. "Congratulations. You're no longer the new Society."

**If they created chaos and Corva escaped:** Laine is satisfied with Harker's return but warns them. "The Lamplighter doesn't forget faces. You've made an enemy. Make sure you're worth the trouble." (Different hook — the Syndicate as recurring antagonist.)

### Tell It To Them Straight (closing)

> The Wet Ember again. Same corner table. The fire is lower now, and the tavern is emptying out as the evening shift heads to the factories. Harker is at the bar, nursing something stronger than tea and scribbling in a new journal. Laine left an hour ago with the evidence.
>

>
> Outside, the Galvanic hum of the factory quarter competes with something fainter — the pulse of the Aether, just past the edge of the old town. Two forces that refuse to share. You're standing in the middle, same as everyone else.
>
> But now you know what's under the ground. And you're starting to wonder what else is hiding in the spaces between.

## 8. Quick Rules Reference for the GM

*Include a condensed one-page reference with the starter adventure, covering only what's needed for this session.*

### Core Mechanic
Roll d100. Equal to or under target = success. Lower is better. Margin under target determines how well you succeed.

### Skill Checks
Target = skill percentage on character sheet. GM sets difficulty by applying modifiers (-10% hard, -20% very hard, +10% easy).

### Non-Skill Ability Checks
When no skill applies, roll d100 under (Attribute + 4) x 10. A BR +1 character rolls under 50%. A SP +2 character rolls under 60%.

### Combat Sequence
1. Lowest PC declares first (tells table what they're doing)
2. Place token on timing track at action's speed
3. Resolve tokens in count order
4. Roll d100 under relevant skill (Firearms, Melee)
5. On hit: roll damage, subtract Soak from armor, apply remainder to HP
6. Check wound tier — penalties apply immediately

### Wound Tiers
| Tier | Status | Penalty |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | OK | None |
| 2 | Harmed | -10% to all rolls |
| 3 | Wounded | -25% to all rolls |
| 4 | Critical | -50% to all rolls, death saves |

### Casting (Simplified for This Session)
1. Declare spell and target
2. Place token at spell's casting time
3. When token resolves, roll d100 under casting target
4. Margin determines tier: 1-10 = Weak, 11-30 = Standard, 31-50 = Strong, 51+ = Spectacular
5. Wild casters: roll 2d100, take the result farther from target (can suppress 0-2 tiers based on skill)
6. Scholarly casters: roll 1d100, can restrict down to any lower tier
7. Pay exhaustion for the tier used

### Zone Effects (for this adventure)
| Zone | Casting | Firearms | Galvanic Devices |
|------|---------|----------|-----------------|
| Neutral (Wet Ember) | Normal | Normal | Normal |
| Galvanic +3 (Foundry) | -6% to target | Normal | +6% reliability |
| Aetheric +4 (Tunnels) | +8% to target | Jam on 85+ | Jam on 75+ |

## 9. Design Notes

### Why This Structure Works

**The three-zone arc is the thesis statement of Aetherfall.** Every player gets a moment where their character shines and a moment where they're disadvantaged. Kael's revolver is king in the foundry; it's a liability in the tunnels. Sera is muted in the Galvanic zone; she's devastating underground. Mira's social skills matter in Act 1 and the negotiation; her arc pistol is a liability in Act 3. Aldric's support magic is useful everywhere but critical in the tunnels.

**The investigation → combat → exploration arc** mirrors how most Aetherfall sessions should feel. Start with questions, escalate to action, end with discovery. The violence is meaningful — it happens because of what the party found, not because it's time for a random encounter.

**Corva as a recurring NPC** regardless of outcome. If she escapes, she's a future antagonist or reluctant ally. If captured, the Syndicate sends someone worse. If the party negotiated, she remembers professionals who could be useful — and dangerous. One session plants seeds for twenty.

### Pre-Gen Balance

The four pre-gens are deliberately asymmetric:
- **Kael** is the most straightforward — point, shoot, reliable. New players who want simple mechanics should take him.
- **Sera** is high-risk, high-reward. Wild casting is exciting but unpredictable. Players who like chaos should take her.
- **Aldric** is the thinker — support magic, investigation, the one who solves problems without violence. Players who like utility should take him.
- **Mira** is the social operator — her sheet looks underpowered in combat but she controls the conversation scenes and carries the investigation. Players who like roleplay should take her.

Every character has at least one scene where they're the most important person at the table. That's the design goal.
