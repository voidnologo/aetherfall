# Skills & Progression Reference

## Document Status: DRAFT — Playtest Ready
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-03

Complete skill list, character creation allocation, and advancement mechanics.

---

## 1. Skills Overview

Every skill is tied to one of the six attributes. Your target number for any skill check:

```
Skill target = ((10 + attribute) × skill level) + 30
```

Without training:

```
Unskilled attempt = (5 + attribute) × 5
```

**Character creation cap:** 3 per skill
**Gameplay maximum:** 5 per skill
**Maximum skills:** 15 per character
**Highest possible target:** (10 + 3) × 5 + 30 = **95** — there is always a chance of failure.

---

## 2. Skill Point Allocation (Character Creation)

Skills are purchased from group pools. Each group's points:

```
Skill points per group = 10 + (sum of attributes in that group)
```

| Group | Attributes | Point Range |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Physical | BR + PC | 4–16 |
| Mental | IN + AW + PW | 1–19 |
| Social | SP | 7–13 |

One skill point buys one skill level. The creation cap of 3 per skill means no single skill costs more than 3 points from its group's pool.

Not all points need to be spent. A combat-focused character may have leftover Social points; a socialite may have leftover Physical points. That's fine — it reflects who your character is.

## 3. Complete Skill List (27 Skills)

### Physical Group — Points: 10 + BR + PC

#### BR — Brawn

| Skill | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Athletics** | Climbing, swimming, jumping, running, lifting — all feats of raw physicality. Covers any physical challenge that isn't fighting. |
| **Brawl** | Unarmed combat. Punches, grapples, headbutts, tackles. Fast and always available — fists don't jam in a Wild Zone. |
| **Melee** | Armed close combat. Swords, axes, clubs, knives, improvised weapons. Covers attack and parry with any handheld weapon. |
| **Intimidation** ★ | Physical menace — looming presence, cracking knuckles, breaking things to make a point. *Flex stat: may be placed under SP instead.* |

#### PC — Physical Coordination

| Skill | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Firearms** | Pistols, rifles, shotguns, automatic weapons. Loading, clearing jams, and shooting under pressure. Separate from Ranged because guns live and die by the Aetheric balance. |
| **Piloting** | Operating vehicles — airships, automobiles, motorboats, motorcycles. Covers routine driving and white-knuckle chases alike. |
| **Ranged** | Bows, crossbows, thrown weapons. Martial ranged combat immune to magic zone interference. The reliable option when the Aether is thick. |
| **Sleight of Hand** | Pickpocketing, palming objects, lockpicking, hiding things on your person. The art of making your hands do things people don't notice. |
| **Stealth** | Moving silently, hiding, shadowing someone, blending into crowds. Getting somewhere without being seen or heard. |

### Mental Group — Points: 10 + IN + AW + PW

#### IN — Intellect

| Skill | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Craft** | Working with your hands — metalsmithing, woodworking, leatherwork, repair, fabrication. If you're building or fixing a physical object, this is the skill. |
| **Engineering** | Machines, structures, electrical systems, demolitions. Understanding how complex things work — and how to make them stop working. |
| **Medicine** | First aid, diagnosis, surgery, pharmacology, poisons. Treating wounds, identifying diseases, and knowing which end of the syringe to use. |
| **Occult Lore** | Magical theory, supernatural history, ritual knowledge, identifying enchantments. Knowing what you're dealing with before you deal with it. Not casting — understanding. |
| **Science** | Chemistry, physics, forensics, biology, geology. Analytical thinking applied to the material world. The lab coat skills. |
| **Tactics** | Reading a battlefield, planning ambushes, coordinating teams, anticipating enemy moves. The difference between a fight and a strategy. |

#### AW — Awareness

| Skill | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Empathy** | Reading people — body language, tone of voice, sensing motives, detecting lies. Knowing what someone feels, even when they're hiding it. |
| **Investigation** | Crime scenes, research, connecting clues, deductive reasoning. Active analysis — finding what's there and figuring out what it means. |
| **Observation** | Noticing details — spotting the glint of a wire, hearing footsteps above, catching the odd detail in a room. Passive alertness and active searching. |
| **Survival** | Tracking, hunting, navigation, foraging, shelter-building, reading weather. Everything between the city limits and the horizon. |

#### PW — Power

| Skill | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Resolve** | Resisting mental effects, maintaining composure under pressure, enduring pain and fear, shaking off stun. Your refusal to break. Every character benefits — not just casters. |
| **Scholarly Casting** | Learned magic. Requires a spellbook and study. You choose your tier, control your power, and accept lower backlash risk. See *The Two Paths of Magic*. |
| **Wild Casting** | Intuitive magic. No spellbook — you reach into the Aether and take what comes. Higher ceiling, higher risk, less control. See *The Two Paths of Magic*. |

### Social Group — Points: 10 + SP

#### SP — Social Prowess

| Skill | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Deception** | Lying, disguise, forgery, acting, misdirection. Making people believe things that aren't true — with words, appearance, or documents. |
| **Etiquette** | High society, formal situations, cultural knowledge, diplomacy. Knowing which fork to use, how to address a baroness, and when to shut up. |
| **Intimidation** ★ | Social pressure — veiled threats, cold authority, weaponized silence. *Flex stat: may be placed under BR instead.* |
| **Leadership** | Inspiring, commanding, rallying, organizing. Getting people to follow you — in a firefight, a boardroom, or a revolution. |
| **Persuasion** | Convincing, negotiating, charming, haggling. Changing minds through argument, appeal, or sheer likability. |
| **Streetwise** | Underworld contacts, black market access, reading a neighborhood, knowing who runs what. The opposite of Etiquette — knowing which alley to take and who to ask. |

> ★ **Intimidation** appears in both Physical and Social. When you take this skill, choose which attribute defines it for your character and buy it from that group's points. A bruiser who looms (BR) and a spymaster who whispers threats (SP) both intimidate — differently.

## 4. Progression

### 4.1 Earning XP

**1 XP per session** — automatic. You showed up, you played, you're growing.

**+Bonus XP from table vote** — At session's end, the table recounts the session together. Memorable scenes, clever solutions, heroic moments, great roleplay, advancing the story — the group recognizes standout moments and awards a bonus XP to players who earned one. Multiple players can earn a bonus in the same session, everyone can, or no one might. Not every session will have standout moments, and that's fine. The automatic XP means everyone is always growing.

**Typical rate: 1–2 XP per session.** No XP for kills. No XP for loot. The advancement system doesn't know combat exists.

### 4.2 Improving Skills

Cost to advance a skill to level N = **N XP**.

| Advance | Cost | Cumulative from 0 |
|---------|------|--------------------|
| 0 → 1 | 1 XP | 1 XP |
| 1 → 2 | 2 XP | 3 XP |
| 2 → 3 | 3 XP | 6 XP |
| 3 → 4 | 4 XP | 10 XP |
| 4 → 5 | 5 XP | 15 XP |

- **Character creation cap:** 3 per skill
- **Maximum:** 5 per skill (gameplay advancement only)
- **Highest possible target:** (10 + 3) × 5 + 30 = **95** — there is always a chance of failure

**Pacing from creation cap to mastery (3 → 5):** 9 XP — roughly 5–9 sessions of focused investment in a single skill. Mastery is a campaign arc, not a weekend.

**New skills:** Learning something new (0 → 1) costs just 1 XP, but should have a narrative justification — a mentor, downtime study, or a story moment that sparked it.

**Learning new skills after character creation** is not limited by the original group point pools. XP is a universal currency. A soldier who befriends a caster during the campaign can spend 1 XP to pick up Occult Lore at level 1 — no Mental group points required. The group pools only govern initial allocation.

### 4.3 Improving Attributes

**10 XP per +1 to an attribute.** Cap remains **+3**.

This is expensive — 5–10 sessions of saving everything for a single point. But the payoff is cascading: every skill under that attribute improves when the attribute does. Getting stronger makes you better at Athletics, Brawl, *and* Melee. Growing more mentally resilient deepens your EP *and* your casting *and* your Resolve.

Attribute growth is the rare, landmark moment of a campaign. It changes who your character fundamentally *is*.

### 4.4 Pacing Reference

At 1–2 XP per session, here's what different investments feel like:

| Goal | Cost | Sessions |
|------|------|----------|
| Pick up a new skill (0 → 1) | 1 XP | 1 |
| Get competent at something (1 → 2) | 2 XP | 1–2 |
| Reach trained level (2 → 3) | 3 XP | 2–3 |
| Become an expert (3 → 4) | 4 XP | 2–4 |
| Achieve mastery (4 → 5) | 5 XP | 3–5 |
| Master a skill from creation cap (3 → 5) | 9 XP | 5–9 |
| Raise an attribute by 1 | 10 XP | 5–10 |
| Build a brand new skill to expert (0 → 4) | 10 XP | 5–10 |

### 4.5 Design Philosophy

**No XP for violence.** The progression system cannot see whether you fought, ran, negotiated, or hid. It rewards showing up and being memorable. This is the strongest anti-murderhobo signal a system can send — the optimal strategy for advancement is to play your character well, not to seek combat.

**Cheap to try, expensive to master.** Picking up a new skill costs 1 XP — almost nothing. This encourages characters to grow from story moments. Your grizzled soldier starts learning Occult Lore after a terrifying encounter with a Veil entity? That's 1 XP and a great character moment. But taking that same skill to level 5 is a 15 XP commitment over many sessions. Breadth is cheap; depth is earned.

**Attributes are landmarks.** At 10 XP per point, raising an attribute is a campaign event — the culmination of sessions of sustained investment. But the cascading effect (every skill under that attribute improves) makes it transformative. A character who raises BR from +1 to +2 doesn't just get stronger — their Athletics, Brawl, and Melee all improve. Their HP per tier increases. They are fundamentally different.

**Players make meaningful choices.** With 1–2 XP per session and multiple avenues for spending, every point matters. Do you push your best skill toward mastery? Pick up something new the story demands? Save for an attribute increase? The scarcity comes from opportunity cost, not raw cost.
