# Art Direction — Aetherfall

## 1. Logo Design Brief

### The Game
Aetherfall is a tabletop RPG set in a 1920s-era world torn between two forces: the **Aether** (magic, mystery, the old world) and the **Engine** (technology, industry, the new world). The tagline is **"When the Aether Fell, Everything Changed."**

### Visual Identity
The logo needs to work across: web header, character sheets, landing page hero, print materials, and social media. It should feel like it belongs on the cover of a leather-bound rulebook in a world where jazz clubs coexist with ward-makers and arc pistols.

### Design Pillars

**1. The Duality**
The game's core tension is Aetheric vs. Galvanic — magic vs. technology, old vs. new, mystery vs. industry. The logo should express this tension visually. Not one side winning — the balance between them.

**2. Era**
1920s industrial meets arcane. Think: Art Deco geometry, industrial lettering, gaslight-era ornament — but with an otherworldly edge. Not purely Deco, not purely fantasy. The intersection.

**3. Color Palette**
The site uses two signature colors throughout:
- **Aether:** `#3dc8e0` — cyan/teal, cool, magical
- **Galvanic:** `#e8a825` — amber/gold, warm, industrial

The logo should work in:
- Full color (Aether + Galvanic on dark background `#080b14`)
- Single color (white on dark, or dark on light for print)
- Small scale (favicon, 32px) and large scale (hero banner)

**4. Typography Reference**
The site uses:
- **Playfair Display** — serif, for headings (elegant, editorial)
- **Source Serif 4** — body text (readable, warm)
- **Cormorant Garamond** — labels and small caps (refined, classical)

The logo can use custom lettering but should feel harmonious with these typefaces.

**5. Tone**
- Serious but not grim
- Elegant but not precious
- Industrial but not steampunk-cliché (no gears-on-everything)
- Mysterious but not horror
- The word "Aetherfall" itself is evocative — the Aether _fell_, like rain, like a collapse, like something that changed everything

### What the Logo Should NOT Be
- Generic fantasy (swords, dragons, shields)
- Pure steampunk (cogs, goggles, brass everything)
- Horror/dark (skulls, blood, grimdark)
- Overly complex (must read at 32px)
- A literal illustration — it's a wordmark/logotype with optional iconography

### Suggested Directions to Explore

**Direction A: Split Wordmark**
"AETHERFALL" in strong Art Deco lettering, with a visual split or gradient — the left side trending toward Aether (cool, flowing, slightly ethereal) and the right side trending toward Galvanic (warm, structured, slightly industrial). The transition point is the tension.

**Direction B: Monogram + Wordmark**
A stylized "A" or "AF" monogram that embodies the duality (one half magical, one half mechanical), paired with the full "AETHERFALL" wordmark below. The monogram doubles as a favicon.

**Direction C: The Fall**
"AETHERFALL" with the letters subtly descending or fracturing — evoking the moment the Aether fell and the world broke. Particles or energy fragments drifting from the text. Restrained, not chaotic.

**Direction D: Sigil**
A circular or shield-shaped emblem that contains both Aetheric and Galvanic symbols (the Veil and the Engine) in tension. "AETHERFALL" arcs around or sits below. Works as a stamp, seal, or badge.

### Deliverables Needed
1. **Primary logo** — Full wordmark, horizontal, for web header and landing page
2. **Icon/monogram** — Square, works at 32px as favicon and at 512px as app icon
3. **Print variant** — Single-color version for character sheets and print materials
4. **Dark background variant** — For the site (dark theme, `#080b14` background)
5. **Light background variant** — For print and character sheets (white background)

### Format Requirements
- SVG preferred (scalable, can be themed with CSS)
- PNG fallbacks at 1x and 2x for web
- Favicon: ICO or PNG at 32x32 and 180x180 (Apple touch icon)

## 3. Prompt Engineering Notes

When using an AI art generator, consider these prompt anchors:

**For the logo:**
- "Art Deco logotype, 1920s industrial meets arcane, dual-tone cyan and amber"
- "Elegant wordmark, dark background, magical and technological tension"
- "AETHERFALL text logo, Playfair Display inspired, split between ethereal blue and industrial gold"
- Reference styles: Art Deco poster typography, Bioshock Infinite UI, Dishonored logo treatment, Peaky Blinders title card

**For the duality concept:**
- Left/cool side: flowing energy, faint runes, misty edges, cyan glow
- Right/warm side: sharp geometry, faint circuitry, metallic edges, amber glow
- The meeting point: where magic and machine collide — crackling, vibrant, alive

**Negative prompts / things to avoid:**
- No photorealistic elements
- No generic fantasy tropes (swords, shields, dragons)
- No steampunk clichés (exposed gears, goggles, top hats)
- No horror elements
- Keep it clean and scalable — logo, not illustration
