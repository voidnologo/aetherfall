**Date:** 2026-04-06
**Goal:** Design the world's narrative context — factions, history, current state of affairs, Adventuring Societies, zone politics, and voice guide for the web chapter. Produce a complete design doc that establishes all setting parameters.

## Overview

Created `docs/requirements/WORLD_DESIGN.md` — a 9-section design document establishing the narrative layer of the setting. The world is balkanized and fragmented (Diamond Age / Snow Crash / Arcane influences), with magic having physically reshaped geography, collapsed governments, and created a patchwork of local power centers. Stories start local, rumors outpace facts, and adventure is close.

The doc covers design principles, history (Before/Eruption/Counter-Response/Now), rumors and wonders, three meta-factions as ideological currents, eight interlocking sample factions, Adventuring Societies as the player interface to the world, zone politics, a four-voice guide for the web chapter, and ten open questions for future sessions.

## Key Design Decisions

- **No world name, no canonical geography.** The world is "the world." No named countries, continents, or cities. GMs fill in their own.
- **Balkanized, not at war.** The world is fragmented into local power centers — phyles, enclaves, communes — not organized into nation-states or global powers. Inspired by Diamond Age / Snow Crash.
- **Magic physically reshaped geography.** The eruption didn't just add magic — it transformed regions. Farmland became mystical forest. City districts collapsed and rebuilt around magical living.
- **Meta-factions are ideological currents, not organizations.** Restoration, Awakening, and Arrangement are directions people lean, not groups with membership cards.
- **Factions are purely narrative.** No mechanical reputation scores or standing systems. Fits the rules-lite philosophy.
- **Multiple Societies per faction.** The Society ecosystem is dense — a single patron may charter several Societies, creating intra-faction rivalry alongside cross-faction competition.
- **Four-voice web chapter.** Player-facing content will use multiple in-world contributors (Handler, Scholar, Street, Believer) influenced by Shadowrun 3e's BBS format.
- **Society content may need its own chapter.** It bridges world-setting and player-facing rules — a different kind of content than lore.
