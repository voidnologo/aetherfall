## Overview

Created the full plan for Story 03, "The Lamplighter's Price." The story follows from Story 02's seeds: Corrigan Fels blackmails the Society into retrieving lost Syndicate cargo from the deep tunnels beneath the Ashworth Foundry. What they find — the same transformative agency from the mill, operating beneath a Galvanic factory — reframes the world's central mystery. 8 chapters, ~10,000-12,000 words, rotating POV.

Key narrative choices: urban/underground setting contrasts with Story 02's wilderness expedition; Fels's coercion is blackmail (not a favor or debt collection) driven by the Society costing him during the Ashwick Job; the Kael-Mira trust dynamic cracks open when Kael calls her information filtering; Sera's accumulation accelerates with a vision she keeps secret; Aldric connects the mill and tunnels into a pattern that questions the world's fundamental polarity.

## Key Design Decisions

- **"The Lamplighter's Price" as Story 03:** Follows the earliest planted seed (Fels/Syndicate from the Ashwick Job) and returns to the foundry tunnels. Urban setting contrasts with Story 02's wilderness. The mystery comes home.
- **Fels as blackmailer, not debt collector:** The Society doesn't owe Fels anything. He's using information leverage because they cost him. This is coercion, not commerce.
- **8 chapters vs Story 02's 10:** Tighter, faster structure for a contained urban story. ~10-12k words vs Story 02's ~18k.
- **The mill-tunnel connection:** Same agency, same materials, radically different zone environments. This is the story's big revelation — whatever is remaking these structures doesn't obey the Aether/Engine polarity.
- **Kael-Mira trust crack:** First open acknowledgment of Mira's information filtering. Not a rupture — a statement of position that will develop across future stories.
- **Sera's secret vision:** First time Sera keeps something from the team. The network/convergence beneath the city is a major seed for future stories.
